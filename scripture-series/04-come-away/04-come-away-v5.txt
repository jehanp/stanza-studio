Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I shuttered all the windows facing seaward,
pulled the curtains thick against the grey.
Told myself the fog was just a season —
I'd learn to live with my face turned away.

But through the glass your voice came steady, low and clear,
warm as a lighthouse burning offshore through the mist:
"The fog has lifted, love — the sea is glittering —
come out and feel the morning that you missed."

[Pre-Chorus]

The blossoms shook themselves from winter's keeping,
the tide has turned the shore a deeper green.
The birds are singing through the salt-stung branches —
a sound I hadn't let myself believe.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the grey is gone, the fog has cleared today.
Open up your shuttered heart,
love has crossed the water from the start.
Come away, come away —
the sea is wide and waiting for you today.

[Verse 2]

I pressed my palm against the salt-filmed window,
heard the harbour breathing past the glass.
The gulls were wheeling low above the water
beneath a sky too open now to last.

You didn't call out twice, you didn't pull my hand —
you stood outside the fog as if you knew.
And something sealed behind my ribs remembered
that there was never any wall between us two.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the tide is in, the grey has had its day.
Love stood steady on the shore,
you don't have to face the fog alone no more.
Come away, come away —
the morning's wide awake and here to stay.

[Bridge]

I was the fog that hid the harbour from itself,
you were the lighthouse burning through my doubt —
patient as the tide returning every season,
steady till the grey all cleared it out.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the mist has gone and I can't make it stay.
I'm stepping through the shutters I pulled tight —
love was never far, love was always light.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready — I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
