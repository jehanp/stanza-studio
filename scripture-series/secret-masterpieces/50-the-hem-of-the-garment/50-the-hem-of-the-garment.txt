The Hem of the Garment

Indie Folk / Fingerstyle Acoustic

[Verse 1]
Twelve years of bleeding, twelve years in the dark,
and every doctor left another mark.
The money's gone, the body's getting thin,
and nobody will let me touch their skin.

[Verse 2]
I heard he's passing through the marketplace,
and people press around him for the space.
If I could reach the bottom of his clothes,
the border where the tassel meets the road — I'd know.

[Chorus]
I only need the hem — the very edge,
the lowest thread upon the lowest ledge.
I'm not asking for the center or the throne,
just let me touch the fringe and I'll go home.

[Verse 3]
I pushed between the bodies, reached my hand,
and felt the fabric brush across the sand.
The bleeding stopped like someone shut the door,
and twelve long years of hemorrhage was no more.

[Chorus]
I only need the hem — the very edge,
the lowest thread upon the lowest ledge.
I'm not asking for the center or the throne,
just let me touch the fringe and I'll go home.

[Bridge]
He turned and asked: who touched me in this crowd?
The power left me — who has drawn it out?
I fell down trembling, told him everything,
and he said: daughter, go — your faith's the string.

[Final Chorus]
I only touched the hem — the very edge,
the lowest thread upon the lowest ledge.
I wasn't asking for the center or the throne,
but he turned around and called me by my own.

[Outro]
The fringe was all I had the faith to reach,
and even that was more than enough to teach.
