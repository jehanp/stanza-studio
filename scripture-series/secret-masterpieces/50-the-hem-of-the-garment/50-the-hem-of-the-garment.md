# The Hem of the Garment — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Mark 5:25-34
**Key Verse:** _"She had heard the reports about Jesus and came up behind him in the crowd and touched his garment. For she said, 'If I touch even his garments, I will be made well.' And immediately the flow of blood dried up... Jesus said to her, 'Daughter, your faith has made you well; go in peace.'"_

---

## Core Theme

The sufficiency of desperate, imperfect faith — you don't need access to the center; even the edge of something real is enough to change everything.

## Lyric Angle

A woman who has been bleeding for twelve years — ritually unclean, socially untouchable, financially ruined by doctors — hears Jesus is passing through. She doesn't ask for an audience. She doesn't call out. She reasons: if I can just touch the hem (the tzitzit, the tassels at the border of his garment), that will be enough. She pushes through a crowd (illegal — she would make everyone she touches unclean) and grazes the fringe. Instantly healed. But Jesus stops. "Who touched me?" He won't let her remain anonymous. She falls down and tells the whole truth. He calls her "daughter" — the only person in the Gospels he addresses this way. The lyric is for anyone whose faith is barely a whisper, who feels too unclean for the center, too spent for a proper approach — and discovers that even the margin of something real is enough.
