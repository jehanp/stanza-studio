The Broken Jar

Singer-Songwriter Acoustic / Chamber Folk

[Verse 1]
The jar has shattered on the stony floor,
and cannot hold the water anymore.
I gather up the pieces in my hand,
and weep upon the dry and dusty land.

[Verse 2]
The clay was dry and hardened in the sun,
and everything I built had been undone.
The wheel was spinning fast and out of line,
and ruined all the beauty of design.

[Chorus]
But look, the fingers do not cast away,
the ruins of the dry and broken clay.
They gather up the dust and add the rain,
to work it on the spinning wheel again.

[Verse 3]
I thought the crack was final and the end,
and nothing was remaining left to mend.
But now the water makes the texture soft,
and other hands are lifting me aloft.

[Chorus]
But look, the fingers do not cast away,
the ruins of the dry and broken clay.
They gather up the dust and add the rain,
to work it on the spinning wheel again.

[Bridge]
The spinning wheel is turning day and night,
and molding us inside the fading light.
It doesn't throw the shattered cup aside,
but shapes a vessel where we can abide.

[Final Chorus]
But look, the fingers do not cast away,
the ruins of the dry and broken clay.
They gather up the dust and add the rain,
to make a vessel beautiful again.

[Outro]
The second shape is rising from the wheel,
and quiet, gentle mercy has been sealed.
