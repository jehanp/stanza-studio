# The Threshing Floor — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Ruth 3:6-9
**Key Verse:** _"She went down to the threshing floor… She came softly and uncovered his feet and lay down… 'Spread your wings over your servant, for you are a redeemer.'"_

---

## Core Theme

Vulnerability as the doorway to belonging — the terrifying act of uncovering yourself completely, and the grace of being claimed rather than rejected.

## Lyric Angle

Ruth's act is astonishing: a foreign widow with no status walks alone to a man's threshing floor at midnight, uncovers his feet, and asks to be claimed. It's maximum vulnerability with no guarantee of acceptance. The lyric honours the risk of showing yourself fully — without pretense, without title, without leverage — and discovering that the one you approached has already decided in your favour. The subplot of the nearer kinsman who refuses (Ruth 4:6) adds texture: not everyone is willing to pay the cost. But the true redeemer removes the sandal — sealing the covenant publicly.
