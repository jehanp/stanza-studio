The Threshing Floor

Acoustic Soul / Neo-Folk

[Verse 1]
The harvest moon is hanging low and bright,
and I have come uncovered in the night.
I lay my body down upon the grain,
and wait for recognition to remain.

[Verse 2]
The barley has been gathered from the field,
and every hidden motive now revealed.
I have no title, only dusty feet,
and place myself beneath your linen sheet.

[Chorus]
Spread your covering over what is bare,
and claim the one who chose to meet you there.
The threshing separates the gold from chaff,
but love decides which portion is your half.

[Verse 3]
They say a woman shouldn't risk this much,
or show herself dependent on a touch.
But nothing in the daylight offered home,
so midnight is the only field I roam.

[Chorus]
Spread your covering over what is bare,
and claim the one who chose to meet you there.
The threshing separates the gold from chaff,
but love decides which portion is your half.

[Bridge]
The nearest kinsman turned and walked away,
too cautious for the price he'd have to pay.
But you removed your sandal at the gate,
and bought the one the world considered late.

[Final Chorus]
Spread your covering over what is bare,
and lift the one who risked it all to care.
The threshing separates the gold from chaff,
and love has written us into its graph.

[Outro]
The morning finds us spoken for and known,
no longer gleaning in the field alone.
