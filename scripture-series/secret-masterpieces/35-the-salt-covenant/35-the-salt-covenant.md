# The Salt Covenant — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** 2 Chronicles 13:5 / Numbers 18:19
**Key Verse:** _"Do you not know that the LORD, the God of Israel, gave the kingship over Israel forever to David… by a covenant of salt?"_

---

## Core Theme

Promises that cannot decay, dissolve, or be revoked — the idea of an indestructible bond in a world littered with broken contracts.

## Lyric Angle

In the ancient Near East, sharing salt sealed an unbreakable covenant because salt does not rot, corrode, or decompose. The lyric contrasts the modern world's disposable promises — contracts with escape clauses, vows made with fingers crossed, relationships abandoned when convenient — against a bond that has a mineral permanence. The "salt" is never named as divine or religious; it is presented as a mysterious preservative baked into the foundation of a commitment, something chemical that outlasts every attempt to dissolve it.
