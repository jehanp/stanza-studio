The Salt Covenant

Industrial Folk / Dark Electronic

[Verse 1]
The treaties crumble like the autumn leaves,
and every signature eventually deceives.
The ink has bled and contracts turn to dust,
corroded by the oxidation of our trust.

[Verse 2]
We shook on deals that rotted at the core,
and learned that paper promises are poor.
The handshake faded with the afternoon,
and every solemn pledge dissolved too soon.

[Chorus]
But there is salt inside the covenant made,
a mineral that does not rust or fade.
It cannot spoil, it cannot be undone,
it preserves the bond until the final sun.

[Verse 3]
They said forever but they meant for now,
and dressed their exit as a sacred vow.
The world is littered with abandoned oaths,
like shipwrecks wearing decorative clothes.

[Chorus]
But there is salt inside the covenant made,
a mineral that does not rust or fade.
It cannot spoil, it cannot be undone,
it preserves the bond until the final sun.

[Bridge]
No acid eats through crystal packed so tight,
no season turns the compound into blight.
The maker mixed it in before the start,
and pressed it deep into the hardest part.

[Final Chorus]
So there is salt inside the covenant made,
a crystal that will never decompose or trade.
It cannot spoil, it cannot come undone,
a permanent preserving under any sun.

[Outro]
The table where we shared the salt still stands,
and binds us tighter than a thousand hands.
