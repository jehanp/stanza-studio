The Narrow Gate

Post-Punk / Dark Wave

[Verse 1]
The wide road stretches easy to the eye,
and everybody walking passes by.
The signs are lit with comfortable light,
and no one has to squint or strain their sight.

[Verse 2]
But tucked between two buildings, hard to see,
a doorway barely wide enough for me.
No crowd is pressing through this alley door,
and those who find it say they've found much more.

[Chorus]
The narrow gate is harder than you think,
it strips the luggage down to every link.
You cannot carry kingdoms through this space,
you enter single-file and face to face.

[Verse 3]
I left the avenue of easy praise,
and traded all the width for narrower days.
The passage scraped my shoulders on both sides,
and took the extra person that I hide.

[Chorus]
The narrow gate is harder than you think,
it strips the luggage down to every link.
You cannot carry kingdoms through this space,
you enter single-file and face to face.

[Bridge]
Few there are who even see the frame,
and fewer still who call it by its name.
The wide road promises you everything,
but narrows at the end to suffering.

[Final Chorus]
The narrow gate is smaller than your pride,
and only honest bones can fit inside.
You cannot carry kingdoms through this space,
but what you find within will fill the place.

[Outro]
The door is thin, the life beyond is wide,
and everything that matters waits inside.
