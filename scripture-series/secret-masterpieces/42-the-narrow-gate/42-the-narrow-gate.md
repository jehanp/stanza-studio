# The Narrow Gate — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Matthew 7:13-14
**Key Verse:** _"Enter by the narrow gate. For the gate is wide and the way is easy that leads to destruction, and those who enter by it are many. For the gate is narrow and the way is hard that leads to life, and those who find it are few."_

---

## Core Theme

The counterintuitive truth that constraint leads to freedom — the harder path is the one that opens into real life.

## Lyric Angle

Everyone pictures a gate as an entrance to something. Jesus inverts the intuition: the attractive, spacious, popular path narrows to destruction, while the cramped, overlooked, unpopular entrance opens into life. The lyric makes the narrow gate physical — an alley door you have to squeeze through, that scrapes your shoulders, that won't let you carry your baggage. The "luggage" is ego, image, accumulated identities. The wide road is the life of easy consensus — going where everyone goes, believing what costs nothing. The narrow gate demands you come stripped. The turn: the door is thin, but the life behind it is vast.
