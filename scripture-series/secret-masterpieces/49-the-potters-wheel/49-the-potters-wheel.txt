The Potter's Wheel

Trip-Hop / Downtempo

[Verse 1]
The clay was spinning underneath his hands,
and I could feel the pressure of his plans.
The vessel that he started first went wrong,
so he pressed it down and started on a song.

[Verse 2]
I watched him crush what I thought I would be,
and reshape every contour that was me.
It felt like ruin — this collapsing in,
but he was just removing where I'd been.

[Chorus]
Can the clay say to the potter: what's your aim?
Can the vessel ask the wheel to stop the frame?
The hands that press are hands that also hold,
and what collapses now is being re-enrolled.

[Verse 3]
The jar I was before had cracks and leaks,
and couldn't hold the water for the meeks.
He didn't patch the flaws or paint them bright,
he started over — fresh and new and right.

[Chorus]
Can the clay say to the potter: what's your aim?
Can the vessel ask the wheel to stop the frame?
The hands that press are hands that also hold,
and what collapses now is being re-enrolled.

[Bridge]
Go down to the potter's house and see,
the way the maker handles what will be.
The vessel marred becomes a vessel planned,
for nothing is beyond his second hand.

[Final Chorus]
Can the clay say to the potter: you've gone wrong?
The spinning wheel says otherwise — stay on.
The hands that press are hands that also shape,
and what was crushed is taking better drape.

[Outro]
The wheel slows down, the vessel holds its form,
and what was reshaped weathers every storm.
