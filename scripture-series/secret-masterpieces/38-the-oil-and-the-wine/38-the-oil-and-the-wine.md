# The Oil and the Wine — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Luke 10:33-35 (The Good Samaritan)
**Key Verse:** _"But a Samaritan, as he journeyed, came to where he was, and when he saw him, he had compassion. He went to him and bound up his wounds, pouring on oil and wine."_

---

## Core Theme

Healing that arrives from the person you least expected — the outsider who stops when the qualified walk past.

## Lyric Angle

The parable's sting is in who helps and who doesn't. The priest crosses the road. The Levite crosses the road. The hero is the ethnic and religious outsider — the one with no obligation, no reputation to maintain, no credential. The lyric modernises the road: a highway shoulder, a ditch, a vehicle pulling over. The "oil and wine" are ancient wound-care — antiseptic and soothing — but in the lyric they become any act of costly, unasked-for care. The bridge carries the Samaritan's stunning instruction to the innkeeper: "Whatever more you spend, I will repay." Unlimited liability taken on behalf of a stranger.
