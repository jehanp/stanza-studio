The Oil and the Wine

Vintage Soul / Gospel-Tinged R&B

[Verse 1]
They left me on the shoulder of the track,
with gravel in my lungs and bruises on my back.
The ones who should have stopped just looked away,
and crossed the painted median to stay.

[Verse 2]
The collar and the robe walked by my side,
too occupied with schedules and their pride.
They measured out the risk of getting stained,
and calculated all they stood to gain.

[Chorus]
But someone pulled their vehicle aside,
and knelt beside the ditch where I had cried.
They poured the oil and wine into the cut,
and lifted me before the door was shut.

[Verse 3]
A foreigner with no allegiance here,
without a title or a volunteer.
They wrapped my ribs in cotton, soft and white,
and drove me through the remnants of the night.

[Chorus]
But someone pulled their vehicle aside,
and knelt beside the ditch where I had cried.
They poured the oil and wine into the cut,
and lifted me before the door was shut.

[Bridge]
They left their name and number at the desk,
and paid the bill without a single ask.
Whatever more it costs I'll pay on return,
a debt of mercy we could never earn.

[Final Chorus]
So someone pulled their vehicle aside,
and knelt beside the wreck where I had died.
They poured the oil and wine into the ache,
and healed the wound the righteous failed to take.

[Outro]
The ones who passed were fluent in the law,
but love was spoken by the one they saw as raw.
