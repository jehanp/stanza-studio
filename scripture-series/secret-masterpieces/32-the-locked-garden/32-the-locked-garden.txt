The Locked Garden

Trip-Hop / Downtempo Electronic

[Verse 1]
Behind the iron gate a fountain hides,
where nothing uninvited ever rides.
The petals open only to the keeper's key,
a bloom reserved for just one pair to see.

[Pre-Chorus]
They tried to climb the hedge and steal a rose,
but thorns protect the beauty that it knows.
No merchant buys, no stranger takes by force,
the garden keeps its own and stays on course.

[Chorus]
A locked enclosure, sealed against the trade,
where nothing genuine was ever weighed.
A spring enclosed that only pours for one,
beneath a canopy of private sun.

[Verse 2]
The marketplace is selling what they found,
and chopping sacred orchards to the ground.
They package up the intimate and rare,
and auction off what should be handled with care.

[Pre-Chorus]
They tried to climb the hedge and steal a rose,
but thorns protect the beauty that it knows.
No merchant buys, no stranger takes by force,
the garden keeps its own and stays on course.

[Chorus]
A locked enclosure, sealed against the trade,
where nothing genuine was ever weighed.
A spring enclosed that only pours for one,
beneath a canopy of private sun.

[Bridge]
The north wind blows to spread the spice abroad,
a voluntary fragrance, never clawed.
The keeper knows the combination well,
and enters through a gate no coin could sell.

[Final Chorus]
A locked enclosure, safe against the trade,
where nothing precious ever needs to fade.
A spring enclosed that only pours for one,
an orchard ripening beneath our sun.

[Outro]
The garden waits behind its bolted gate,
for hands that never hurry, never late.
