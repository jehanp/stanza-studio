The Alabaster Box

Neo-Soul / Jazz Ballad

[Verse 1]
She brought the jar she'd been saving up for years,
and cracked the neck in front of all the peers.
The oil ran down like rivers without end,
and everybody watched but couldn't comprehend.

[Verse 2]
They said the waste was criminal and wrong,
three hundred silver pieces — gone like song.
But she had calculated what he's worth,
and poured out everything she had on earth.

[Chorus]
Break the jar, let the fragrance fill the room,
don't save it for the living or the tomb.
What good is perfume locked inside a vase,
when love is sitting right here, face to face?

[Verse 3]
The critics at the table shook their heads,
and tallied up the cost of what she shed.
They only saw the money on the floor,
but she was anointing something so much more.

[Chorus]
Break the jar, let the fragrance fill the room,
don't save it for the living or the tomb.
What good is perfume locked inside a vase,
when love is sitting right here, face to face?

[Bridge]
Wherever this story gets retold,
her name is woven into every fold.
She gave what couldn't be given by degrees,
and worshiped in a way that brought her to her knees.

[Final Chorus]
Break the jar — don't calculate the cost,
what's poured in love is never truly lost.
The fragrance fills the centuries and stays,
and what they called a waste becomes the praise.

[Outro]
The room still smells of what she gave that night,
and extravagance is love done right.
