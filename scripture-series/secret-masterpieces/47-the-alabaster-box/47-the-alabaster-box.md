# The Alabaster Box — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Mark 14:3-9
**Key Verse:** _"She broke the flask and poured it over his head... 'Why was the ointment wasted like that?'... But Jesus said, 'Leave her alone... She has done a beautiful thing to me... wherever the gospel is proclaimed in the whole world, what she has done will be told in memory of her.'"_

---

## Core Theme

The scandal of extravagant, uncalculated devotion — when love refuses to be reasonable or measured.

## Lyric Angle

A woman enters a dinner party with an alabaster jar of pure nard — worth a year's wages. She breaks it (not opens, breaks — the jar can never be resealed) and pours the entire thing over Jesus' head. The room erupts in outrage: this could have been sold, given to the poor. Jesus silences them: "She has done a beautiful thing." The lyric captures the tension between economic logic and reckless love. The critics are not wrong about the math — they're wrong about what matters. The woman understood something no one else in the room did: some moments demand everything, and holding back is the real waste. The broken jar is the point — love that can be recapped is not love at full measure.
