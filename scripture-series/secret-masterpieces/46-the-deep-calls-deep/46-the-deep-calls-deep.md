# The Deep Calls Deep — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Psalm 42:7-8
**Key Verse:** _"Deep calls to deep at the roar of your waterfalls; all your breakers and your waves have gone over me. By day the LORD commands his steadfast love, and at night his song is with me."_

---

## Core Theme

The paradox that the deepest suffering leads to the deepest encounter — that being overwhelmed is sometimes the only way to reach what lies beneath the surface.

## Lyric Angle

Psalm 42 is written by someone in exile, cut off from the temple, surrounded by mockers asking "where is your God?" The psalmist is drowning — "all your breakers and waves have gone over me." But then this extraordinary phrase: "deep calls to deep." The abyss within the person is calling out to the abyss of God. The lyric captures the experience of being pulled under — depression, displacement, spiritual drowning — and discovering that at the very bottom, something responds. The waterfalls aren't just punishment; they're the route down to where the real encounter happens. The surface was all distraction. The deep is where the living water actually flows.
