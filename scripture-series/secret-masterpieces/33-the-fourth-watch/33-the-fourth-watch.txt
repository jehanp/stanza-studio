The Fourth Watch

Post-Punk Revival / Atmospheric Rock

[Verse 1]
The oars are breaking and the current pulls,
against the tired muscles of the hull.
We rowed since evening, inching through the foam,
and every stroke is further out from home.

[Verse 2]
The second hour collapsed into the third,
and we have run entirely out of word.
The lantern sputtered twice and then went black,
and all we do is strain against the slack.

[Chorus]
But in the fourth and final watch of night,
a figure crosses on the grey and white.
The waves obey a step we can't explain,
and something walks on top of all our pain.

[Verse 3]
At first we thought it was the ghost of grief,
returning just to finish our belief.
We shouted at the phantom on the crest,
but all it said was: put your fear to rest.

[Chorus]
But in the fourth and final watch of night,
a figure crosses on the grey and white.
The waves obey a step we can't explain,
and something walks on top of all our pain.

[Bridge]
He did not come at one or two or three,
but waited till we'd emptied every plea.
The final hour when surrender comes,
is when the horizon walks and overcomes.

[Final Chorus]
But in the fourth and final watch of night,
the stranger climbs aboard and holds us tight.
The gale submits to feet we can't explain,
and all that walked above us stills the strain.

[Outro]
The calm arrives when rowing cannot save,
and someone sovereign steps across the wave.
