# The Midnight Oil — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Matthew 25:1-13
**Key Verse:** _"Then the kingdom of heaven will be like ten virgins who took their lamps and went to meet the bridegroom. Five of them were foolish, and five were wise… And at midnight there was a cry, 'Here is the bridegroom! Come out to meet him.'"_

---

## Core Theme

The things that sustain you in the long wait cannot be borrowed at the last minute — readiness is the slow, private work of preparation.

## Lyric Angle

The parable of the ten virgins is brutal in its simplicity: five brought extra oil, five didn't. When the bridegroom arrives at midnight — much later than expected — the five without oil beg to borrow, but the wise say no: there won't be enough for both of us. While the foolish run to buy, the door shuts. The harshest line: "I do not know you." The lyric applies to anyone who has coasted on borrowed energy, borrowed faith, borrowed readiness — relying on proximity to prepared people rather than doing the quiet, unglamorous work of filling their own reserves. Oil is whatever you tend in private that sustains you when the crisis comes: character, discipline, inner life.
