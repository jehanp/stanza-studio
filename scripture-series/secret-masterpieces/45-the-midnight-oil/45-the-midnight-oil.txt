The Midnight Oil

Americana / Country Soul

[Verse 1]
Ten of us were standing at the gate,
with lanterns lit and nothing left but wait.
The bridegroom said he'd come but gave no time,
and half of us were burning on a dime.

[Verse 2]
Five of us had extra in the flask,
and five had only what the moment asked.
When midnight struck and still he hadn't come,
the wicks were dying one by sputtering one.

[Chorus]
You cannot borrow what was meant to burn,
the oil inside your lamp is what you earn.
When midnight comes and every light grows thin,
the ones who saved enough will still get in.

[Verse 3]
They begged us: share a little from your store,
but what we carried wasn't built for four.
We said: go buy your own before it's late,
and while they ran, the door closed on the wait.

[Chorus]
You cannot borrow what was meant to burn,
the oil inside your lamp is what you earn.
When midnight comes and every light grows thin,
the ones who saved enough will still get in.

[Bridge]
It sounds like cruelty — the shutting door,
the voice that says I do not know you more.
But readiness was never about the gift,
it's what you tended in the long night's drift.

[Final Chorus]
You cannot borrow what was slowly grown,
the fire inside your lamp is yours alone.
When midnight comes and every eye is dim,
the ones who kept their flame will walk with him.

[Outro]
The door has shut, the feast has now begun,
and readiness was everything or none.
