# The Sealed Fountain — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Song of Solomon 4:12-16
**Key Verse:** _"A garden locked is my sister, my bride, a spring locked, a fountain sealed. Your shoots are an orchard of pomegranates... Awake, O north wind, and come, O south wind! Blow upon my garden, let its spices flow."_

---

## Core Theme

The sacred privacy of intimacy — some things are more precious because they are not available to everyone.

## Lyric Angle

The lover in Song of Solomon describes his beloved as a locked garden, a sealed fountain — not because she is imprisoned, but because her deepest self is reserved, guarded, offered only to the one who holds the key. The lyric speaks to anyone who has learned to protect their inner world from casual consumption — creativity, vulnerability, spiritual depth, romantic intimacy. In a culture that demands everything be public and accessible, the sealed fountain is a radical act: the refusal to scatter what is sacred. The turn comes when the beloved herself invites the wind to carry her fragrance out — intimacy is not hoarding, it is choosing.
