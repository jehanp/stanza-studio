The Charcoal Fire

Warm Acoustic Indie Folk / Singer-Songwriter

[Verse 1]
The shadows gathered in the open yard,
and keeping watch was getting cold and hard.
I stood beside the brazier in the grey,
and three times blew the only name away.

[Verse 2]
The bitter ash was settling on my sleeve,
and there was nothing left but just to leave.
I ran into the wet and salty night,
and tried to blind myself to morning light.

[Refrain]
The cold coal burns the memory in the chest,
and will not let the heavy shadow rest.

[Chorus]
But look, another fire is on the beach,
just past the point where waves began to reach.
The smoke is rising in the golden air,
and someone warm is calling over there.

[Verse 3]
A breakfast cooking on the open coal,
to mend the tattered edges of the soul.
No list of sins to carry and repeat,
just simple words to tell us: come and eat.

[Chorus]
But look, another fire is on the beach,
just past the point where waves began to reach.
The smoke is rising in the golden air,
and someone warm is calling over there.

[Bridge]
I didn't wait to guide the heavy boat,
I threw myself into the sea afloat.
The water washed the courtyard from my skin,
and let the quiet mercy now begin.

[Final Chorus]
And see, the fire is burning on the beach,
right where the salty tide can never reach.
We sit together in the morning air,
with nothing but a silent blessing there.

[Outro]
The courtyard fire is cold and out of sight,
and morning has absorbed the longest night.
