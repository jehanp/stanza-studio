# Secret Masterpieces — Lyric Plan

> A suite of 15 new songs drawn from lesser-known, deeply poetic scriptures — each lyric built on consecutive end-rhymes (AABB) and layered with hidden spiritual meaning.

---

## Overview

These songs are rooted in scriptures that rarely appear in congregational worship but carry some of the Bible's most arresting imagery. The lyric methodology follows the same embedded approach as the Scripture Series — no direct quotation, no overt religious vocabulary — but leans harder into metaphor, surprise, and the kind of line that lands differently on a second read.

**Key lyric constraint:** Every couplet uses consecutive end-rhymes (AABB). No alternating ABAB patterns. The rhyme should feel inevitable, not forced.

**No Sinhala translations.** English only.

---

## Conversion Methodology

### Step 1 — Sit With the Obscurity
- Choose the less-travelled verse. Read why it was overlooked.
- Let the strangeness of the image — a white stone, a breakfast fire, a valley of trouble turned door — sit without explanation.

### Step 2 — Find the Human Nerve
- Strip the theological layer and ask: what universal wound or wonder does this touch?
- That nerve becomes the invisible spine of the song.

### Step 3 — Build With Consecutive Rhymes
- Draft every section so that adjacent lines share end-sounds (AABB, CCDD).
- Rhyme must carry meaning — if a couplet rhymes but doesn't earn its turn, rewrite it.

### Step 4 — Hide the Source
- The scripture should be unrecognisable to a secular ear but unmistakeable to someone who knows the text.
- Test: read the lyric to someone unfamiliar with the passage — it should move them. Read it to someone who knows the passage — it should wreck them.

### Step 5 — Compress
- Cut every word that doesn't bleed. These songs reward density.

## Rhyme Audit Checklist

Before marking any song complete, verify:

- [ ] Every pair of adjacent lines in each verse ends with matching sounds
- [ ] Every chorus couplet rhymes on the stressed final syllable
- [ ] Bridge couplets follow the same AABB pattern
- [ ] No forced rhyme sacrifices the meaning of the line
- [ ] The scripture source is invisible to a secular reader
- [ ] The scripture source is unmistakeable to someone who knows the text
