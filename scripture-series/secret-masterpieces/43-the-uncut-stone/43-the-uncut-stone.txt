The Uncut Stone

Industrial Folk / Stomp Rock

[Verse 1]
They built their towers high with polished rock,
and carved their idols from the finest stock.
But in the distance, cut by no one's hand,
a boulder started rolling through the land.

[Verse 2]
No chisel ever shaped its ragged face,
no architect had drawn it into place.
It gathered speed without a human push,
and came at kings like wind through burning bush.

[Chorus]
The uncut stone will shatter every throne,
and break the iron mixed with brittle bone.
No empire made of gold or silver stands,
when what was never built by human hands
comes crashing down like thunder on the plains,
and fills the earth with what of it remains.

[Verse 3]
The statue with its head of golden pride,
and chest of silver, legs of iron wide,
stood tall above the nations in its dream,
until the stone undid it at the seam.

[Chorus]
The uncut stone will shatter every throne,
and break the iron mixed with brittle bone.
No empire made of gold or silver stands,
when what was never built by human hands
comes crashing down like thunder on the plains,
and fills the earth with what of it remains.

[Bridge]
They never saw it coming from the hill,
a kingdom not erected by the will
of emperors or armies or their swords,
but growing like a mountain without words.

[Final Chorus]
The uncut stone becomes a mountain wide,
and fills the earth from every broken side.
No empire made of gold or silver stands,
the future belongs to what no one planned.

[Outro]
The statue crumbles into wind and dust,
and what was never forged survives the rust.
