# The Uncut Stone — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Daniel 2:34-35, 44-45
**Key Verse:** _"A stone was cut out by no human hand, and it struck the image on its feet of iron and clay, and broke them in pieces... But the stone that struck the image became a great mountain and filled the whole earth."_

---

## Core Theme

The unstoppable power of what is not manufactured — the thing no human system built is the thing that outlasts every empire.

## Lyric Angle

Nebuchadnezzar's dream is one of scripture's most cinematic images: a towering statue representing successive empires (gold, silver, bronze, iron, clay) — and a single stone, cut without hands, rolling from a mountain and shattering the whole structure. The stone then grows into a mountain that fills the earth. The lyric is for anyone watching powerful systems that seem permanent — institutions, ideologies, cultural empires — and wondering if anything can challenge them. The answer is not a bigger empire. It's something unmanufactured, unchiseled, unsponsored — something that moves without a marketing budget and outlasts every regime because it was never built by the hands that build things that fall.
