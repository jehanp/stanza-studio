The Refiner's Flame

Alternative Metal / Prog-Metal Ballad

[Verse 1]
The furnace has been lit beneath the floor,
and temperatures are rising at the core.
I thought the heat was punishment for sin,
but something else is being forged within.

[Verse 2]
The dross is lifting to the molten face,
as impurities are floating into space.
I burn but I am not consumed to ash,
instead the gold is showing through the gash.

[Chorus]
Who can endure the day the fire descends,
and separates the alloy from the blend?
The silversmith is watching for a sign,
until his own reflection fills the shine.

[Verse 3]
The crucible is narrow and severe,
and every cheap addition disappears.
The counterfeit I carried in my bones,
dissolves beneath the pressure and the moans.

[Chorus]
Who can endure the day the fire descends,
and separates the alloy from the blend?
The silversmith is watching for a sign,
until his own reflection fills the shine.

[Bridge]
He does not leave the metal in too long,
or let the temperature become too strong.
The moment that his image shows up clear,
he pulls us from the kiln and draws us near.

[Final Chorus]
Who can endure the day the fire descends,
and burns away the things we thought were friends?
The silversmith has waited for the sign,
and now his own reflection fills the shine.

[Outro]
The furnace cools, the ore has been refined,
and what remains is everything designed.
