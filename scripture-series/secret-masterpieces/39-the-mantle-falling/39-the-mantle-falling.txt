The Mantle Falling

Indie Orchestral / Epic Folk

[Verse 1]
The chariot of fire tore the sky in half,
and split the riverbed along its path.
I watched the one who taught me disappear,
and all that's left is fabric landing here.

[Verse 2]
The cloak descended through the burning air,
and settled heavy on my shoulders bare.
It carries every promise and command,
of someone who once struck the Jordan sand.

[Chorus]
The mantle falls on those who didn't choose,
and asks them if they're ready now to lose.
The weight of what was carried hits the floor,
and we must decide to pick it up once more.

[Verse 3]
I tore my shirt in two along the seam,
and traded my old garment for the dream.
The river that he parted with his voice,
is standing still and waiting for my choice.

[Chorus]
The mantle falls on those who didn't choose,
and asks them if they're ready now to lose.
The weight of what was carried hits the floor,
and we must decide to pick it up once more.

[Bridge]
Where is the power that was in his stride,
the source that made the stubborn waters divide?
I struck the bank with borrowed cloth in hand,
and watched the current separate on command.

[Final Chorus]
The mantle falls on those who stayed behind,
inheriting a mission unconfined.
The weight of what was carried fills the core,
and what was done through one is done through more.

[Outro]
The sky has closed, the chariot has passed,
but everything it carried still holds fast.
