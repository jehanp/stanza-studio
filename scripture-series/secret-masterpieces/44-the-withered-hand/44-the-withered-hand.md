# The Withered Hand — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Mark 3:1-6
**Key Verse:** _"And he said to the man with the withered hand, 'Come here.' And he said to them, 'Is it lawful on the Sabbath to do good or to do harm?'... He said to the man, 'Stretch out your hand.' He stretched it out, and his hand was restored."_

---

## Core Theme

The demand to expose your brokenness publicly — healing that refuses to be hidden or polite.

## Lyric Angle

A man with a withered hand sits in a synagogue. The religious leaders are watching Jesus — not out of hope for the man, but to trap Jesus in a Sabbath violation. Jesus doesn't quietly heal. He calls the man to the center. He asks the room a question they refuse to answer. Then he tells the man to stretch out the very thing he's been hiding. The healing is deliberately public, deliberately confrontational. The lyric speaks to anyone whose damage has become something they conceal — who has learned to keep their broken parts tucked under a coat. The call is: bring it out. Don't wait for privacy. Don't wait for permission. Stretch it out in front of the very people who benefit from you staying small.
