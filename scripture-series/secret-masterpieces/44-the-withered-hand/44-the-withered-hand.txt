The Withered Hand

Lo-Fi Indie / Bedroom Pop

[Verse 1]
My right hand curled up years ago in shame,
and no one in the room would say my name.
I kept it hidden underneath my coat,
a secret that I carried like a note.

[Verse 2]
They watched to see if something would be done,
not hoping for my healing — wanting one
good reason to accuse the hands that heal,
and prove the rules mattered more than what I feel.

[Chorus]
Stretch out your hand — the one you learned to hide,
the one that curled and withered up inside.
Don't wait until the room is cleared of eyes,
the healing happens right where judgement lies.

[Verse 3]
He didn't pull me to a private space,
or whisper low to save the public face.
He said it loud in front of every stare:
stretch out the thing you're most ashamed to bare.

[Chorus]
Stretch out your hand — the one you learned to hide,
the one that curled and withered up inside.
Don't wait until the room is cleared of eyes,
the healing happens right where judgement lies.

[Bridge]
The watchers wanted rules above the skin,
and cared more for the day than who was in
the kind of pain that cripples at the root,
but love will always trump the letter's suit.

[Final Chorus]
Stretch out your hand — the one that shame withdrew,
and watch it open up like something new.
The room is full of eyes that want you small,
but stand and stretch and heal in front of them all.

[Outro]
The hand uncurls, the fingers find their reach,
and what was dead is living now to teach.
