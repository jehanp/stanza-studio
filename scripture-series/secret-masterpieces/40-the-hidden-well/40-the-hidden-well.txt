The Hidden Well

Desert Rock / Psychedelic Folk

[Verse 1]
The jealous hands have packed the wells with earth,
and filled with stones the springs that gave us birth.
They could not bear to see the water flow,
so buried every source we used to know.

[Verse 2]
My father dug these channels long ago,
and named them for the promises below.
But enemies have plugged them in the night,
and claimed the ground was always dry and tight.

[Chorus]
So dig again where others tried to kill,
and clear the ancient rubble from the sill.
The living water hasn't moved or dried,
it's waiting just beneath what they have tried.

[Verse 3]
They quarreled over every hole I dug,
and said the spring belonged to every thug.
I moved along and found another plot,
and named it room enough for what we've got.

[Chorus]
So dig again where others tried to kill,
and clear the ancient rubble from the sill.
The living water hasn't moved or dried,
it's waiting just beneath what they have tried.

[Bridge]
The wells of our ancestors still remain,
beneath the jealous gravel and the pain.
Rehoboth — there is space enough to thrive,
for everything they buried is alive.

[Final Chorus]
So dig again through every lie they spilled,
and find the ancient aquifer unfilled.
The living water surges from the deep,
a promise that was never theirs to keep.

[Outro]
The stone is cleared, the pressure starts to pour,
and what was blocked is overflowing more.
