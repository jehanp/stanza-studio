# The Dry Bones Valley — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Ezekiel 37:1-10
**Key Verse:** _"He said to me, 'Prophesy over these bones, and say to them, O dry bones, hear the word of the LORD… I will cause breath to enter you, and you shall live.' … And the breath came into them, and they lived and stood on their feet, an exceedingly great army."_

---

## Core Theme

Resurrection from the most hopeless state imaginable — the audacity of speaking life into what every rational observer has declared permanently dead.

## Lyric Angle

God takes Ezekiel to a valley full of bones — not fresh corpses, but dry, sun-bleached, ancient bones. "Can these bones live?" is the question. Ezekiel's answer is brilliant: "You know." Not yes, not no — just deference to the one asking. Then God tells him to prophesy — to speak to the bones. The process is staged: first structure returns (bone clicking to bone, the rattling), then flesh covers them, but they remain lifeless. Only when Ezekiel prophesies to the wind (breath/spirit) do they come alive. The lyric applies to anyone standing over what looks completely dead — a relationship, a dream, a community, their own inner life — being asked: will you speak to this? Will you call for the breath?
