The Hermon Dew

Neo-Classical Ambient Piano

[Verse 1]
The dry grass is begging and starting to die,
under the weight of the summer-burned sky.
The earth is cracked open in silent despair,
and heat is the only thing left in the air.

[Verse 2]
But quietly, softly, before the first light,
a cool mist descends from the safety of night.
A blessing that gathers on leaf and on stem,
completing the valley like some hidden gem.

[Chorus]
The dew is of Hermon, it falls on the hill,
so silent and perfect, so tender and still.
It heals every shadow of poison and pain,
and gives us the strength to be living again.

[Verse 3]
Like lilies that open and stretch to the sky,
we lift up our heads and we no longer sigh.
The roots are dug deep in the forest of green,
releasing the beauty that hasn't been seen.

[Chorus]
The dew is of Hermon, it falls on the hill,
so silent and perfect, so tender and still.
It heals every shadow of poison and pain,
and gives us the strength to be living again.

[Bridge]
No thundering rain and no howling of wind,
the anger of seasons has finally thinned.
A whisper of moisture that covers the ground,
where peace is the only sweet thing to be found.

[Final Chorus]
The dew is of Hermon, it falls on the hill,
so silent and perfect, so tender and still.
It heals every shadow of poison and pain,
and gives us the strength to be living again.

[Outro]
The morning has come and the garden is green,
nourished by blessings we haven't yet seen.
