# The Watchman's Tower — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Habakkuk 2:1-3
**Key Verse:** _"I will take my stand at my watchpost and station myself on the tower… For still the vision awaits its appointed time; it hastens to the end — it will not lie. If it seems slow, wait for it; it will surely come; it will not delay."_

---

## Core Theme

Patient vigilance when the answer is overdue — the discipline of watching when everyone else has abandoned the post.

## Lyric Angle

Habakkuk's posture is extraordinary: he doesn't storm off, doesn't demand an immediate answer, doesn't give up. He physically stations himself on a tower and says, "I will watch to see what He will say to me." The lyric inhabits that sustained posture — the person who has been waiting for something promised, mocked by others for their persistence, watching a road where nothing has appeared for years. The pivot is the reassurance: the vision has an appointed time. It will not lie. It will not be late. The call is not to force the answer but to hold the post until it arrives on its own schedule.
