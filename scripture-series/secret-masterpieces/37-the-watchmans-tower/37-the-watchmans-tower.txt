The Watchman's Tower

Ambient Post-Rock / Cinematic Instrumental Vocal

[Verse 1]
I built a station on the highest ridge,
and fixed my telescope across the bridge.
The answer has been overdue for years,
but I refuse to climb down from up here.

[Verse 2]
The neighbors say the vigil is a waste,
and every morning leaves a bitter taste.
They tell me nothing's coming from that road,
and I should just set down the heavy load.

[Chorus]
But I will stand upon the rampart stone,
and wait for what was spoken, though alone.
The vision has a time it must arrive,
and I will keep my lantern burning, still alive.

[Verse 3]
The seasons cycle through the narrow glass,
and caravans of strangers walk and pass.
I mark the days in tallies on the rail,
and scan the dust for movement on the trail.

[Chorus]
But I will stand upon the rampart stone,
and wait for what was spoken, though alone.
The vision has a time it must arrive,
and I will keep my lantern burning, still alive.

[Bridge]
Though it delays, it will not tell a lie,
the appointed hour will not pass us by.
So carve the message plainly on the slate,
that anyone who reads it knows to wait.

[Final Chorus]
So I will stand upon the rampart stone,
and guard the word that I was given on my own.
The vision has a date it must arrive,
and when it comes it will not pause or strive.

[Outro]
The tower holds, the telescope is trained,
and patient watching has not been in vain.
