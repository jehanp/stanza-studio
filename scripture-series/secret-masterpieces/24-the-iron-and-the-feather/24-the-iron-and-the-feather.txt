The Iron and the Feather

Delta Blues / Folk Rock

[Verse 1]
The town is leaning over on its side,
and there is nowhere left for us to hide.
The doorways buckle and the rafters groan,
and cracks are splitting through the oldest stone.

[Verse 2]
They tell us everything is going fine,
and that the crooked walls are out of line.
They rewrite rules to make the tilt seem straight,
and hang the heavy balance on our gate.

[Chorus]
But someone stands beside the leaning wall,
and holds a simple iron in the fall.
A heavy brass that hangs upon a thread,
to show the crooked path that we have led.

[Verse 3]
Against the vertical of string and gold,
we see the lies that we have bought and sold.
The truth is silent and it does not scream,
but breaks the pattern of our drunken dream.

[Chorus]
But someone stands beside the leaning wall,
and holds a simple iron in the fall.
A heavy brass that hangs upon a thread,
to show the crooked path that we have led.

[Bridge]
You cannot bribe the iron on the string,
or change the quiet burden that it brings.
It points directly to the center core,
and measures where we left the level floor.

[Final Chorus]
So stand beside the straight and heavy wall,
and find the iron waiting in the fall.
The simple brass that hangs upon a thread,
will guide the dusty path that we must tread.

[Outro]
The string is hanging straight inside the dust,
and is the only measure we can trust.
