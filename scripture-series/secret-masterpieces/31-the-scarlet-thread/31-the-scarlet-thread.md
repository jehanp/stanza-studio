# The Scarlet Thread — Imagery Notes

**Scripture:** Joshua 2:18 / Genesis 38:28
**Key Verse:** _"Bind this scarlet cord in the window through which you let us down."_

---

## Core Theme

A single fragile lifeline of grace running through the chaos — rescue marked by a thin, red signal that should be too small to matter, but is the only thing that saves.

## Lyric Angle

Rahab is a traitor, an outcast, a woman of the wrong city and wrong profession. Yet the rescue plan hinges on a ribbon — a scarlet cord hung in a window. The lyric traces the idea of an unbroken red thread from the beginning of time, connecting every story of unmerited rescue. The thread is impossibly thin but impossibly strong. It runs through the wreckage of every collapse, every siege, every age — always the same colour, always the same material.
