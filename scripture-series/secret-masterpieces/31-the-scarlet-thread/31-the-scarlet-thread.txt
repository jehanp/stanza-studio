The Scarlet Thread

Indie Americana / Folk Noir

[Verse 1]
The city walls are crumbling into sand,
and no one living here can understand.
A woman ties a ribbon to the frame,
a crimson strip that no one thought to claim.

[Verse 2]
The spies have gone, the soldiers draw their blades,
and march in columns through the morning shades.
But one small house is glowing in the smoke,
protected by a thread the maker spoke.

[Chorus]
A scarlet line is hanging from above,
the thinnest cord of undeserving love.
It runs through every chapter, every age,
a single thread connecting every page.

[Verse 3]
They burned the towers, leveled every street,
and left the rubble underneath their feet.
But nothing touched the window or the sill,
where crimson fabric was suspended still.

[Chorus]
A scarlet line is hanging from above,
the thinnest cord of undeserving love.
It runs through every chapter, every age,
a single thread connecting every page.

[Bridge]
From birth to burial the thread is spun,
through linen cloth and veins that overrun.
It binds the traitor to the holy seat,
and lifts the outcast from the barren street.

[Final Chorus]
A scarlet line is hanging from above,
the only proof of undeserving love.
It runs through every ruin, every page,
a rescue woven into every age.

[Outro]
The cord is thin but nothing cuts it through,
for it was dyed before the world was new.
