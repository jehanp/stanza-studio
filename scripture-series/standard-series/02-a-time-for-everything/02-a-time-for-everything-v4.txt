A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I studied all the star charts in the autumn of my grief,
watched the vine get pruned down hard and stripped of every leaf.
There's a chronicle inside my chest that doesn't care for what I'd prefer —
it just turns, and turns, and turns.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been reading what the night sky tried to write for me,
laughing in the vineyard even as the pruning makes me bleed.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I catalogued the losses like a scholar of the dark,
then watched the vine bear fruit from off the freshly severed bark.
Every constellation that I named had silence on the other face —
every silence held a door I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been pruning what I swore I couldn't spare,
watching something green come back from what was bare.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Charted in one sky and lost beneath the next,
stripped like pruned wood but still carrying the text.
But every ending wrote me to a new beginning —
and the stars keep turning round.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
