A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I dug a furrow in the frost the winter that you left,
watched a thin green thread emerge long after hope bereft.
There's a pendulum inside my chest that swings beyond my will —
it just swings, and swings, and swings.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been clenching fists that need to open wide,
laughing at the table with the ache I hold inside.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to read the season,
I'm still working out the reason —
it isn't mine.

[Verse 2]

I pressed the worn photographs flat, then tore them late at three,
watched a stranger choose the love I thought belonged to me.
Every corner that I turned hid grace upon the other side —
I kept turning 'til I tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been filing down the edges of the stones I kept,
planting seeds in every silence that I should have wept.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to ache and a time to shine.
I don't hold the measure of it,
but there's grace embedded in it
if I don't resign.

[Bridge]

Born and buried underneath the same old bells,
pulled together, pulled apart at the seams.
But every ending was the opening of something further —
further than it seems.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to wound and a time to bind.
I'll release the hand that's turning,
trust the grace that keeps returning,
and believe the light is mine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a purpose and a patience under every sky.
