# Before the Mountains

**Song Number:** 15
**Target Date:** May 1, 2026
**Scripture Source:** Psalm 90:1-17 _(personal reference — not published)_
**Genre:** Dark Folk / Americana
**Suno Tags:** `sentimental, acoustic guitar, dark folk, americana, fingerpicking` — Style Influence 80%

---

### Verse 1

You kept our fathers long before the hill,
from age to age the same unhurried still.
Before the dust was gathered into form,
you were the eye inside the oldest storm.

### Chorus

Count every grain before it joins the sand,
hold every breath like water in the hand.
Let wisdom grow where foolish years have worn,
and make a home of what is left and torn.

### Verse 2

We bloom at dawn and burn before the dusk,
by nightfall we are only scattered husk.
Our years run out the way a sigh runs thin,
a thread pulled loose before we feel the pin.

### Bridge

Return — the morning owes us nothing new,
but still we hold our empty hands to you.
The years you pressed beneath your hand like clay,
restore the shape you meant to give this day.

### Final Chorus

Count every grain before it joins the sand,
hold every breath like water in the hand.
Let wisdom root where seventy years have grown,
and let the work we've done be yours alone.

### Outro

Before the mountains,
you were home.
After the last light,
still not alone.

## Imagery Notes

| Scripture Image | Lyric Translation |
|---|---|
| "Lord, you have been our dwelling place throughout all generations" (v.1) | "You kept our fathers long before the hill" — shelter as something predating geography |
| "Before the mountains were born…from everlasting to everlasting you are God" (v.2) | "long before the hill" / "the same unhurried still" — eternity rendered as an attitude, not a number |
| "Before the dust was gathered into form" | Echo of v.2 and Genesis — creation held as raw material in God's hands |
| "A thousand years in your sight are like a day" (v.4) | "the same unhurried still" — time pressure absent from God's perspective |
| "You sweep them away in the sleep of death; they are like the new grass of the morning — in the morning it springs up new, but by evening it is dry and withered" (v.5-6) | "We bloom at dawn and burn before the dusk / by nightfall we are only scattered husk" |
| "Our years come to an end like a sigh" (v.9) | "Our years run out the way a sigh runs thin" — mortality as a breath becoming inaudible |
| "Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom" (v.12) | "Count every grain before it joins the sand" — numbering reframed as holding sand, already slipping |
| "Return, Lord! How long?" (v.13) | "Return — the morning owes us nothing new" — prayer without entitlement |
| "The years of our life are seventy" (v.10) | "seventy years" — named plainly in the final chorus to anchor the weight of the whole song |
| "Establish the work of our hands" (v.17) | "let the work we've done be yours alone" — offering labor back as belonging to God, not self |
