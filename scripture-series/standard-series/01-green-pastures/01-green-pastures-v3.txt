Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I drifted past the harbor where the foghorns used to cry,
where the lanterns in the windows couldn't reach the clouded sky.
Something steered me through the mist without a chart or star —
there was open water waiting, calm and green and far.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the tide came in without a sound of anger —
it just smoothed the stones and asked the shore to linger.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a banquet in the fog with a place set just for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a jetty where the grey refuses to lift,
and I swore I heard my name inside the salt and the drift.
But a balm I couldn't name laid its warmth against my face,
and the fear that used to shadow me had run out of space.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a banquet in the fog with a place set just for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Balm upon my forehead, from I don't know where —
kindness like a lantern in the thickest harbor air.
Every port I've ever anchored in still holds the light I knew,
every fog I thought was endless burned away and opened new.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a banquet in the clearing and it's laid for you and me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
