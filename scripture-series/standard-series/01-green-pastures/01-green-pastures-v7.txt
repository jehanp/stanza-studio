Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I crossed the line where certainty runs dry,
where neon dims to nothing and the pavement meets the sky.
A current pulled me through a door I hadn't found —
the grass on the other side was cool and thick upon the ground.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the water held the sky without a sound to spare —
as flat and level as a prayer.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
someone set a place for me before I started wandering.
Let the cup run over, let the gift exceed the ask —
I was buried in the valley but I'm rising from the past.

[Verse 2]

There's a corridor where hunger sharpens into prayer,
and the voice that spoke my name felt like it had a care.
An invisible provision lit the way beside my feet,
and the shadow that had followed me found nowhere left to eat.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
someone set a place for me before I started wandering.
Let the cup run over, let the gift exceed the ask —
I was buried in the valley but I'm rising from the past.

[Bridge]

Blessed by hands I couldn't see in the dark between the years —
presence at my shoulder when it cost me more than tears.
Every room that held my leaving still has light behind its door,
every dead-end that I grieved was just a bend before the more.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
someone set a place for us before the world stopped wandering.
Let the cup run over, let the gift exceed the ask —
I was buried in the valley but I'm rising from the past.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
