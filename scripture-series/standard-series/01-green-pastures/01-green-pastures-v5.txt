Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I walked out past the edge of all I'd known,
where the streetlights quit and the concrete turned to stone.
Something steered me through a gate I couldn't see —
there were meadows stretching wide beyond, all waiting there for me.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the water didn't stir, didn't roar, didn't race —
it just rested like a mirror showing grace.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table in the shadow with an empty chair for me.
Fill it till it floods, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a hollow where the daylight never stays,
and I swore I heard my name beneath the shadow's haze.
But a hand I couldn't see set a candle by my side,
and the fear that used to claim me missed its final ride.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table in the shadow with an empty chair for me.
Fill it till it floods, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Oil on my forehead from a hand I couldn't name —
kindness at my shoulder like it knew me when I came.
Every house I've ever left still keeps the candles on,
every trail I thought was dying was just the longer dawn.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table in the shadow and it's set for you and me.
Fill it till it floods, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green meadows in the rearview,
green meadows up ahead.
