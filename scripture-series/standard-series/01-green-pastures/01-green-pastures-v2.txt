Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I wandered off the map where the compass stopped its spin,
where the canyon walls grew quiet and the wilderness moved in.
Something called me through the dark without a lantern or a sign —
there were open spaces out there, breathing, resting, mine.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the rain came down like it wasn't in a hurry —
not to punish and not to warn, not even out of worry.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a campfire in the clearing set for someone just like me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the canyon, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a ridge line where the cold forgets to ease,
and I swore I heard my name beneath the shaking of the trees.
But a warmth I couldn't trace built a shelter by my side,
and the darkness that had trailed me had no place left to hide.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a campfire in the clearing set for someone just like me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the canyon, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Rain upon my forehead, I don't know whose sky —
mercy at my shoulder like it never questioned why.
Every hollow I have sheltered in still holds the warmth I knew,
every trail I thought was ending opened wider into blue.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a campfire in the clearing and it's lit for you and me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the canyon, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
