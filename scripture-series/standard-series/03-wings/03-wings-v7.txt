Wings

Cinematic Pop / Anthemic

[Verse 1]

I climbed until the summit disappeared in cloud,
hands raw against the stone and going blind.
The rock face had a texture like a scripture —
I pressed my cheek to it and fell behind.

[Verse 2]

They said dig deeper, reach your summit, rise above the fear,
but my grip forgot the reason for the climb.
So I clung there to the ledge like it was mercy,
and the ledge wasn't holding me — it was mine.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped climbing,
I stopped reaching,
I let the mountain show me how to rest.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not slipping anymore, I'm not frozen on the face —
I'm descending into freedom on a love that holds my place.
Wings, wings —
I didn't make them, but they're carrying me.

[Verse 3]

The climbers who push hardest often break the rope too fast,
but there's a surer kind of fire in the ones who tend to last.
I waited on the ledge like someone learning how to lean —
turns out the view below the cloud is wider than it seems.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped gripping,
I stopped proving,
I let the wind become my moving.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not clinging anymore, I'm not shaking on the ridge —
I'm soaring past the snowline on a love that builds the bridge.
Wings, wings —
I didn't make them, but they're carrying me.

[Bridge]

They say the strong will reach the top,
they say the fearless will succeed —
but I have been both strong and fearless,
and only grace could meet my need.

[Final Chorus]

And I feel it rise — something underneath,
the everlasting arms of a love beyond belief.
I'm not trembling anymore, I'm not groping in the grey —
I'm lifted past the summits on a love that lights the way.
Wings, wings —
I didn't earn them, but they're carrying me.
