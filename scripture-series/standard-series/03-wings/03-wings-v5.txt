Wings

Cinematic Pop / Anthemic

[Verse 1]

I ran until the river swallowed me,
face down in the shallows, out of breath.
The current had a language like a lullaby —
I let it pull me under, close to rest.

[Verse 2]

They said keep swimming, keep your head up, don't go down,
but my hands forgot the motion of the stroke.
So I floated on the surface like a leaf or like a prayer,
and the water wasn't drowning me — it spoke.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped thrashing,
I stopped fighting,
I let the river carry what I couldn't.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not sinking anymore, I'm not rusting in the rain —
I'm rising from the riverbed on a love that swallows pain.
Wings, wings —
I didn't forge them, but they're carrying me.

[Verse 3]

The ones who race wear out their bones by noon,
but there's a gentler kind of fire under a quieter moon.
I rested in the current like a stone that learned to dream —
turns out surrender isn't losing when it opens like a seam.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped forcing,
I stopped earning,
I let the river do the turning.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not flailing anymore, I'm not choking on the foam —
I'm gliding past the shoreline on a current calling home.
Wings, wings —
I didn't forge them, but they're carrying me.

[Bridge]

They say the swift will take the prize,
they say the brave will never fall —
but I have been both swift and broken,
and grace came when I gave my all.

[Final Chorus]

And I feel it rise — something underneath,
the everlasting arms of a love beyond belief.
I'm not struggling anymore, I'm not gasping in the tide —
I'm carried past the horizon on a love that opens wide.
Wings, wings —
I didn't earn them, but they're carrying me.
