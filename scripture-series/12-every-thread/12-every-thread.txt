Every Thread

Piano Ballad / Chamber Pop

[Verse 1]

You knew me when I was just a whisper in the dark,
before I had a name, before I had a face.
You mapped the nervous system of my laughter,
counted every freckle, memorised the space

between my doubts and what I say out loud.
I've been performing for a faceless crowd —
but you were in the front row all along,
and you already knew the words to every song.

[Chorus]

Every thread, you counted every thread,
every thought before it reached my head.
I'm fearfully, wonderfully undone —
nowhere to hide and nowhere I need to run.
You searched me and you know me,
and you stayed, and you stayed.

[Verse 2]

I wore a hundred faces to a hundred different rooms,
changed the accent, changed the laugh, changed the tune.
But you saw the version underneath the costume,
the midnight-hour edition, the unedited, the true.

If I climbed the highest frequency of heaven,
or I buried myself beneath the basement floor —
you were already there before I landed,
holding out the key to every locked-up door.

[Chorus]

Every thread, you counted every thread,
every bone before the body left the bed.
I'm fearfully, wonderfully unwound —
nowhere to fly where I won't be found.
You searched me and you know me,
and you stayed, and you stayed.

[Bridge]

How precious are the thoughts you think of me —
I tried to count them, but they outnumber the sand.
I wake up every morning still inside the sentence
that you started writing with your hands.

The darkness isn't dark to you,
the night is just the daytime in disguise.
There is no version of me you haven't met,
no shadow deep enough to close your eyes.

[Final Chorus]

Every thread, you counted every thread,
every tear before it left my eyes and bled.
I'm fearfully, wonderfully complete —
no mask, no stage, no need for a retreat.
You searched me and you know me,
and you love me, and you stayed.

[Outro]

You knew me. You knew me.
Before I knew myself, you knew me.
