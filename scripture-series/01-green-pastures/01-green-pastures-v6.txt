Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I stepped beyond the border of the life I recognized,
where the city hum fell silent and the asphalt turned to loam.
An unseen guide led me through a door I hadn't priced —
and the field was already open, soft and calling me to come.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the stream lay still as glass without a rush, without a roar —
a quiet kind of mercy I hadn't seen before.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a lantern in the dark with an empty seat for me.
Let it spill and run and overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm never walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a tunnel where the light refused to go,
and I caught my name on something moving in the undertow.
But an unseen hand lit the flame beside my place,
and the dread that once had claimed me couldn't hold its steady pace.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a lantern in the dark with an empty seat for me.
Let it spill and run and overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm never walking alone.

[Bridge]

Something anointed my forehead though I couldn't find the hand —
goodness at my shoulder like it always had a plan.
Every room I've ever left still has the light on when I roam,
every road I thought was finished was the longer road to home.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a lantern in the dark and it's burning warm for me.
Let it spill and run and overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
