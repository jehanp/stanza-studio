# Scripture-to-Lyrics Conversion Plan

> A bi-weekly plan for transforming Bible scripture into subtle, deeply meaningful song lyrics — April through October 2026.

---

## Overview

Each song is rooted in a specific Bible passage but written so that the scriptural meaning is **embedded subtly** within the lyrics. The goal is not to quote scripture directly but to let its themes, imagery, and emotion flow naturally through poetic language that resonates with any listener — believers and seekers alike.

---

## Conversion Methodology

### Step 1 — Immerse in the Scripture
- Read the full chapter (and surrounding context) multiple times.
- Identify the **core emotion** (hope, lament, wonder, surrender, resolve).
- Note vivid imagery, metaphors, and sensory details in the text.

### Step 2 — Extract the Seed Idea
- Distill the passage down to **one sentence** that captures its heart.
- Translate that sentence into a universal human experience (love, loss, searching, rest, courage).

### Step 3 — Write from the Inside Out
- Never name the scripture or use overtly religious phrasing.
- Use the passage's imagery as a springboard for original metaphors.
- Let the **structure** of the scripture guide the song structure (e.g., a psalm's movement from lament to praise can mirror verse → bridge → chorus).

### Step 4 — Shape to Genre
- Match the emotional arc to the suggested genre.
- Adjust rhythm, vocabulary, and phrasing to fit the musical style.
- Read lyrics aloud to check natural flow and singability.

### Step 5 — Refine and Embed
- Revisit the scripture one final time.
- Ensure at least **3–5 subtle echoes** of the original text survive in the finished lyric (a phrase, a colour, an image, a turning point).
- Polish for economy — every word should earn its place.

## Tips for Keeping It Subtle

1. **Never quote scripture verbatim** — paraphrase, reimagine, translate into modern imagery.
2. **Use the emotion, not the theology** — if the passage is about grace, write about second chances; if it is about creation, write about identity.
3. **Let images do the work** — still waters, morning light, wings, an open door, a table set for one — these carry meaning without explanation.
4. **Write for the listener who doesn't know the source** — the song should move them on its own terms.
5. **Hide the gold** — the scripture reference is a treasure buried in the field. Those who know it will recognise it. Those who don't will still feel its weight.
