Glory Veiled in Dust

Orchestral Alternative Rock

[Verse 1]

The Gardener had planted every root before the field,
the seed was breathing long before its yield.
Each stem and cell and season had its name —
and then the Sower pressed into the soil He'd named.

[Chorus]

Glory buried itself in the garden's dust,
love cracked through the hardened husk of crust.
He bore the weight of every withered and ungrown
and pressed the living water to the stone.

[Verse 2]

Whoever kneels beside the upturned furrow
receives a name that no bloodline could own —
not grafted from the lineage of the bower,
but breathed into the earth and called to grow.

[Chorus]

Glory buried itself in the garden's dust,
love cracked through the hardened husk of crust.
He bore the weight of every withered and ungrown
and pressed the living water to the stone.

[Bridge]

What stirred inside His heartwood grew the world,
a light that kept its green when darkness curled.
He walked the rows He'd sown into the loam,
His own passed over Him without a word or home.
He wove the vine through every wall and wire —
and slipped beneath the soil, unreceived, entire.

[Verse 3]

The Sower pressed His form into the loam of days
and walked the furrows of His own-made ways.
The last seed scattered was the first in ground —
the Gardener took root in what He'd found.

[Final Chorus]

Glory buried itself in the garden's dust,
love cracked through the hardened husk of crust.
He bore the weight of every withered and ungrown
and pressed the living water to the stone.
