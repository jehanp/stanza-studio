Glory Veiled in Dust

Orchestral Alternative Rock

[Verse 1]

The fire had written every metal into shape,
the forge was burning long before escape.
Each element and heat and pressure had its name —
and then the Smelter stepped inside the slag He'd claimed.

[Chorus]

Glory hammered itself into the dust of ore,
love bore the heat that the refining called for.
He took the weight of every failure, every fall,
and carried every fracture down the hall.

[Verse 2]

Whoever brings the raw ore to the threshold
receives the name and form the fire reveals —
not made from the bloodline of the smelter's hall,
but forged in love beneath the hammer's call.

[Chorus]

Glory hammered itself into the dust of ore,
love bore the heat that the refining called for.
He took the weight of every failure, every fall,
and carried every fracture down the hall.

[Bridge]

What burned inside His furnace built the world,
a light that held its form when all was hurled.
He walked the foundry floor of His own making,
His own walked past and never saw the shaking.
He hammered every element in its line —
and passed through all the metal unreceived, refined.

[Verse 3]

The Smelter laid aside the forge and wore the ore
and walked the foundry He had built before.
The last to enter was the first to light the flame —
the Ancient Fire stepped inside the frame He'd named.

[Final Chorus]

Glory hammered itself into the dust of ore,
love bore the heat that the refining called for.
He took the weight of every failure, every fall,
and carried every fracture down the hall.
