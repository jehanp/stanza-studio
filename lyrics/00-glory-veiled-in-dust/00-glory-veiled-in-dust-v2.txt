Glory Veiled in Dust

Orchestral Alternative Rock

[Verse 1]

The Architect had drawn the world before the frame,
the blueprint breathed before the first stone came.
Each line and load and measurement had name —
and then the Drafter stepped inside what He had framed.

[Chorus]

Glory folded itself into the planner's dust,
love shored the wall the structure couldn't trust.
He bore the weight of every crack and shifted seam
and held the building up inside its dream.

[Verse 2]

Whoever brings the deed back to the Drafter
receives the name and title reassigned —
not born from the bloodline of the master,
but breathed into the blueprint by design.

[Chorus]

Glory folded itself into the planner's dust,
love shored the wall the structure couldn't trust.
He bore the weight of every crack and shifted seam
and held the building up inside its dream.

[Bridge]

What rang inside the drafting room became the world,
a light that held its shape when all was hurled.
He walked the halls He'd drawn into their place,
His own walked past without a second glance to trace.
He laid each beam and threaded every joint —
and passed through all He'd built, beside the point.

[Verse 3]

The Architect took on the form He'd sketched in clay
and walked the rooms He'd drafted day by day.
The last to enter was the first to draw the line —
the Author stepped inside His own design.

[Final Chorus]

Glory folded itself into the planner's dust,
love shored the wall the structure couldn't trust.
He bore the weight of every crack and shifted seam
and held the building up inside its dream.
