Wings

Cinematic Pop / Anthemic

[Verse 1]

I walked until my feet went numb from miles,
sat down in the dirt where no one passed.
The cracked earth had a pattern like a story —
I read it with my hands and couldn't last.

[Verse 2]

They said get up, push through, don't let them see you bend,
but my legs forgot the purpose of the road.
So I knelt there in the silence like a question,
and the silence wasn't empty — it was old.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped walking,
I stopped searching,
I let the ground release what I was carrying.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not crumbling anymore, I'm not choking on the dust —
I'm standing on the hilltop on a grace that won't combust.
Wings, wings —
I didn't build them, but they're carrying me.

[Verse 3]

The runners reach the finish and collapse before the crowd,
but there's a deeper kind of fire burning quiet and proud.
I rested in the ashes like a coal that kept its heat —
turns out the ones who kneel are often first upon their feet.

[Pre-Chorus]

I stopped chasing,
I stopped clinging,
I let the stillness do the singing.

[Chorus]

And I felt it rise — something underneath,
a wind I didn't summon lifting higher than belief.
I'm not crawling anymore, I'm not pinned beneath the weight —
I'm walking through the morning on a love that doesn't wait.
Wings, wings —
I didn't build them, but they're carrying me.

[Bridge]

They say the fierce will take the day,
they say the bold will never tire —
but I have been both fierce and fallen,
and mercy met me in the fire.

[Final Chorus]

And I feel it rise — something underneath,
the everlasting arms of a love beyond belief.
I'm not hollow anymore, I'm not stumbling on the stone —
I'm carried past the treeline on a wind that calls me home.
Wings, wings —
I didn't earn them, but they're carrying me.
