# The Weight He Carried (v2)

**Song Number:** 14
**Target Date:** October 12, 2026
**Scripture Source:** Isaiah 53:3-6 _(personal reference - not published)_
**Genre:** Dark Folk / Blues Americana

---

### Verse 1

He walked in like a man who knew the sentence,
shoulders bowed beneath a borrowed coat.
The room had signed the verdict in his absence,
and silence sealed the ink before he spoke.

They looked past him like winter through a window,
a face they learned to file and then forget.
No lifted fist, no argument for pity,
he took the blow and let it pay the debt.

### Verse 2

I watched him lift the ruin with my fingerprints,
the wound that had my name cut to the bone.
He gathered every shard I dropped behind me,
and bore it down the corridor alone.

No witness stand, no counsel for the guilty,
just quiet like a psalm before the rain.
They opened up his side to find the answer,
and found the world inside his mortal pain.

### Refrain

He carried it - he carried it all.

### Chorus

The weight he carried wasn't his -
it was yours, it was mine,
it was every hidden wrong we'd fed
and bolted out of sight.
He wore the bruise, he took the fall,
he bled without a call -
the scars became the medicine,
the breaking healed us all.
Every chain that pinned us to the floor
was shattered to the core -
he was crushed inside the fracture
so the broken found the door.

### Verse 3

They stamped the file as finished, closed, and buried,
and laid him in a loaned and borrowed cave.
But something in the record kept on breathing,
a verdict death could not complete or save.

A woman touched the place that once was fever,
a soldier dropped the hammer in the dust.
The courtroom emptied out into a morning
no language could explain and still be just.

### Refrain

He carried it - he carried it all.

### Chorus

The weight he carried wasn't his -
it was yours, it was mine,
it was every debt we could not pay
driven through the line.
He held the silence like a stone,
he bled but made no sound -
the scars became an anthem,
and the silence shook the ground.
He was pierced so we could stand up whole,
his body bore the cost -
and what they sealed beneath the dark
came back to name the lost.

### Bridge

We scattered like sheep to every private ruin,
each heart convinced its exile was its own.
The guilt, the wreckage, all our chosen violence
was laid upon a friend who stood alone.

### Final Chorus

The weight he carried wasn't his -
we handed him the doom,
from every sin we packed away
and sealed inside the tomb.
He didn't fight, he didn't run,
he let the hammer fall -
and punishment became our peace,
and mercy reached us all.
He pressed the whole world's fracture line
against his faithful chest -
so weary hands could lay it down,
and restless bones could rest.

### Outro

He never raised a word -
the wounds became the sound.
The stone gave back the morning,
and night came unwound.
