A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I planted something in the yard the year you left,
watched it push through frozen ground like it hadn't heard of death.
There's a clock inside my chest that doesn't care what I prefer —
it just turns, and turns, and turns.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been holding on with hands that need to let go,
laughing in the kitchen with tears I'll never show.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I tore the photographs apart, then taped them back at three a.m.,
danced alone at someone's wedding, mourned alone and danced again.
Every war I ever fought had silence on the other side —
every silence held a door I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been gathering the stones I used to throw,
building something from the wreckage, watch it grow.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Born and buried in the same small town,
stitched together, then the seams came down.
But every ending wrote me to the next beginning —
and the wheel keeps spinning round.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
