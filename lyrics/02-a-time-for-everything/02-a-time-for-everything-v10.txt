A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I laid the iron in the forge the year the cold had won,
watched it glow to something workable beneath the morning sun.
There's a bellows in my chest that fans without my will —
it just breathes, and breathes, and breathes.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been pulling metal from the heat before it's ready,
laughing at the anvil when my hammering goes steady.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I took the cracked and broken thing I was and held it to the flame,
let the fire strip the damage till the metal went to claim.
Every cooling brought a silence harder than the steel —
every hardness held a shape I'd finally feel.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been quenching what should stay inside the heat a little longer,
tempering through the process, coming back a little stronger.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Ore lifted from the dark below,
smelted down, poured out, made to glow.
But every breaking gave me back a form that wasn't mine to keep —
and the forge burns while I sleep.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
