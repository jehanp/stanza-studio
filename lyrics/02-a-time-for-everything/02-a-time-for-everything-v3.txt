A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I knelt beside the river in the winter of the year,
watched a sapling push through ice like it refused to disappear.
There's a loom inside my chest that doesn't ask me what I want —
it just weaves, and weaves, and weaves.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been holding threads that need to be surrendered,
laughing through the unraveling with all I've remembered.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I pulled the thread too tight until the tapestry tore through,
then walked into a gallery where someone made it new.
Every current that I fought against had silence on the far shore —
every silence held a door I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been watching where the winter meets the thaw,
reading something in the river and its law.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Woven in this town and frayed in that one,
carried through the winter toward the sun.
But every ending wove me to the next beginning —
and the loom keeps threading on.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
