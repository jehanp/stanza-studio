A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I stood upon the threshing floor the summer of my grief,
watched the wind take what it wanted and return what fell beneath.
There's a millstone in my chest that grinds without my leave —
it just turns, and turns, and turns.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been clutching at the chaff when I should release the grain,
smiling at the harvest table while I'm standing in the rain.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I scattered seed across a field I swore had nothing left to give,
then watched the stubble fields of autumn show me how to live.
Every silence in the granary held a door I hadn't swung —
every song that died in winter would be sung.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been trying to reap before the season for the blade,
building from the stubble while the sowing plans were laid.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Sown in the dark, threshed in the open air,
chaff on the wind and the kernel laid bare.
But every winnowing returned what mattered most to me —
and the field keeps turning free.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
