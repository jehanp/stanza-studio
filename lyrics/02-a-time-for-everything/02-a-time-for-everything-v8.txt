A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I knelt beside the shoreline where you scattered your goodbye,
watched the tide erase your footprints underneath a pewter sky.
There's a compass in my chest that doesn't answer to my need —
it just pulls, and pulls, and pulls.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been standing at the water's edge refusing to retreat,
laughing with the gulls above the place where cold waves meet.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I built a cairn from river stones then watched the storm undo it,
danced into the shallows until the cold ran through it.
Every war I ever fought had stillness on the other side —
every stillness held a tide I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been gathering the shells the ocean broke and left for me,
reading something in the fractures that no one else can see.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Cast out to sea and swept back to the shore,
mended by the salt air, then broken once more.
But every current carried me exactly where I'd need to be —
and the tide keeps rolling free.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
