A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

The seed I pressed into the dark the day you disappeared
sent roots beneath the frozen ground and quietly reappeared.
There's a heartbeat not my own that drums beneath my will —
it just drums, and drums, and drums.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been trying to unknot the grief I tied around myself,
finding small threads of daylight waiting on the shelf.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to bleed and a time to bind.
I'm not given the agenda —
every ending is a mender
working blind.

[Verse 2]

I stayed too long inside a story that had already been told,
danced at funerals and weddings 'til the difference got old.
Every question I surrendered found an answer on the side —
every answer was a tide I could not ride.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been laying down the weapons I inherited for years,
watching something like a garden rise up through the tears.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to go and a time to stay behind.
I don't choose the wind that moves me,
but I'm letting it undo me,
line by line.

[Bridge]

Same small earth that caught my first and final breath,
made and unmade as the seasons turn.
Every fire that burned me down lit the next beginning —
and I'm learning not to mourn.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to find.
I will rest inside the turning,
trust the love that keeps returning,
and release the need to know the reason every time.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season holding purpose, just beneath the noise.
