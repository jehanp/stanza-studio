A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I buried something in the soil the spring you walked away,
watched it push up through the frost like death had lost its say.
There's a clock inside my chest that heeds no wish of mine —
it just turns, and turns, and turns.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been gripping things I know I need to release,
smiling in the hallway while my heart won't find its peace.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to shatter and a time to mend.
I don't choose the chapter,
but I'm learning every rapture
has an end.

[Verse 2]

I crumpled all the letters up, then smoothed them out by dawn,
wept alone at someone's table, stood back up and carried on.
Every fight I ever fought had stillness on the other side —
every stillness held a gate I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been lifting up the stones I used to throw away,
building something from the rubble piece by piece each day.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to scatter and a time to find.
I don't get to name the measure,
but there's beauty in the pressure
if I leave it behind.

[Bridge]

Born and buried in the same worn ground,
pulled together 'til the seams unwound.
But every door that shut wrote me a new beginning —
and the wheel keeps spinning round.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to grieve and a time to climb.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll give myself to the ages
and trust the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a purpose underneath each sky.
