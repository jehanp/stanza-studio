A Time for Everything

Soul / R&B Ballad

[Verse 1]

I buried seeds in soil I thought was dead and done,
watched them break through frozen ground before I thought they'd run.
There's a pendulum inside my chest that never asks me twice —
it just swings, and swings, and swings.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been clutching at the rope that needs releasing,
laughing through the burning even as the smoke is creasing.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to break and a time to bind.
I don't get to pick the season,
but I'm learning that the reason
isn't mine to find.

[Verse 2]

I burned the letters at the stove, then grieved the ash they made,
stumbled into new beginnings while the ruins were on display.
Every wall I ever built had silence on the other side —
every silence held a door I hadn't tried.

[Pre-Chorus]

I've been lifting stones I used to hide beneath,
planting something in the wreckage, watch it breathe.

[Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to lose and a time to find.
I don't get to choose the measure,
but there's purpose in the pressure
if I let it unwind.

[Bridge]

Sown in one field, harvested in the next,
ruins written into restoration's text.
But every ending swung me to a new beginning —
and the pendulum keeps swinging round.

[Final Chorus]

There's a time, there's a time —
a time to weep and a time to shine.
I won't fight the turning pages,
I'll surrender to the ages
and believe the timing's fine.

[Outro]

Everything, everything —
a season and a reason under every sky.
