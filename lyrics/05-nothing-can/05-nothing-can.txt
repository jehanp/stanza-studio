Nothing Can

Alternative Rock / Post-Punk Revival

[Verse 1]

They said the miles would make us thin,
said quiet rust would eat the skin.
They mailed their threats like iron rain,
then watched our doorway bloom with pain.

In February, plaster kissed the floor,
I learned how grief can bolt a door.
They hissed, "It's done; this story ends" -
I said, "You have not met the End who mends."

[Pre-Chorus]

Each hammer swung to break our frame
was forged by mercy into flame.

[Chorus]

Nothing can, nothing can —
not the grave, not the divide,
not the dark where terrors hide,
not the powers, not the plans,
not the nails in human hands.
Nothing can, nothing can —
you could stack the whole world on this love,
and it would stand on scarred pierced hands.

[Verse 2]

Not height, not depth, not heaven's span,
not tomorrow's iron caravan.
Not throne nor ruler, bright or grim,
not nameless dread that stalks the rim.

I was called before I found my voice,
declared as righteous by Another's choice.
What hunts me now arrives too late,
Love learned my name before my date.

[Pre-Chorus]

If all of this is bent toward grace,
then even ruins hold His trace.

[Chorus]

Nothing can, nothing can —
not the grave, not the divide,
not the dark where terrors hide,
not the powers, not the plans,
not the nails in human hands.
Nothing can, nothing can —
you could burn the whole house down around this love,
and it would stand on scarred pierced hands.

[Bridge]

I am convinced - not maybe, not pretend,
I am convinced the Lamb will get the end.
No height, no depth, no world, no nation
can edit blood-sealed reconciliation.

[Final Chorus]

Nothing can, nothing can —
not the death, not the breath,
not the crown, not the cross,
not the wound, not the win,
not the first, not the last within.
Nothing can, nothing can —
you could throw every weapon ever forged at us,
and Love would stand when stars turn dust.

[Outro]

Nothing. Nothing. Nothing can.
