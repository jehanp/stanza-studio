# The Lucky Ones

**Song Number:** 11
**Target Date:** August 31, 2026
**Scripture Source:** Matthew 5:3-12 (The Beatitudes) _(personal reference — not published)_
**Genre:** Indie Pop / Folk-Pop

---

### Verse 1

Here's to the ones with the empty bank accounts,
the ones whose spirits hit the floor.
Here's to the ones still crying in the bathroom
after everyone has walked out the door.

Here's to the quiet ones who never raise a fist,
who inherit what the loud ones leave behind.
Here's to the ones still starving for a justice
that the rest of us pretend we'll someday find.

### Chorus

Oh, you're the lucky ones — yeah, you're the lucky ones,
the last in line, the overlooked, the underdone.
The world forgot to save a seat for you,
but the table's turning, and the gold is coming through.
You're the lucky ones.

### Verse 2

Here's to the ones who show up with the mercy,
who forgive the thing that can't be undone.
Here's to the clean hearts cracking in a dirty room,
still believing in a world that hasn't come.

Here's to the ones who make the peace nobody asked for,
who stitch the wound while everyone takes sides.
Here's to the ones they slander and they drag through mud —
your names are written somewhere the mud can't hide.

### Chorus

Oh, you're the lucky ones — yeah, you're the lucky ones,
the meek, the mourning, the hungry and the stunned.
You didn't get the crown, you didn't get the stage,
but your story's being written on a different page.
You're the lucky ones.

### Bridge

Rejoice — yeah, I know it sounds absurd —
rejoice when they drag your name across the dirt.
Every prophet, every dreamer, every fool who dared to care
got the same raw deal, the same unanswered prayer.
But the reward is in the aching,
and the kingdom's in the break —
you're inheriting the earth
with every hit you choose to take.

### Final Chorus

Oh, you're the lucky ones — you're the golden, chosen ones,
the poor, the pure, the persecuted sons.
The world looked right through you like you were made of glass,
but the first shall be the last, and the last, the last —
the last are the lucky ones.

### Outro

_(claps and gang vocals)_
Lucky, lucky, lucky ones —
every single one.
