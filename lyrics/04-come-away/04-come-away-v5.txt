Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I latched the shutters in November,
nailed the porch boards, cold December.
Said no warmth would find me in this hall —
let the ivy creep along the wall.

But there you stood beside the garden gate,
your voice like sunlight arriving late:
"The turtledoves are back along the wire —
come see the field wake up from winter's fire."

[Pre-Chorus]

The fig tree's branches bow with green,
the blossoms show what cold had screened.
The vines have crept past every stone —
a garden won't stay still alone.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the winter's gone, the grey gave way.
Open up your bolted chest,
love has come to give you rest.
Come away, come away —
you've been hiding long enough today.

[Verse 2]

I traced the mortar with my thumb
and heard the meadow's soft low hum.
The paired doves cooed along the eave
beneath a sky that learned to breathe.

You didn't rush me, didn't pull,
you only waited, patient, full.
And something tight inside my throat
remembered how to sing one note.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the winter's gone, the grey gave way.
Love was patient at your door,
you don't have to hide no more.
Come away, come away —
the flowers came while you were away.

[Bridge]

I was the locked and bolted room,
you were the light that crept past every gloom —
slipping under every door of me
until there was no dark left to keep.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the season turned, I couldn't stay.
I'm stepping out beyond the wall I've made —
love was never far, love never strayed.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
