Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I drew the curtains shut in winter,
swept the garden path to splinter.
Said the cold would keep the ache at bay —
let the turtledoves all fly away.

But there you called from past the cedar row,
your voice a lantern in the evening's glow:
"The hills are green, the flowers fill the lane —
come see the world that came back after rain."

[Pre-Chorus]

The vines are heavy on the wall,
the figs are ripe enough to fall.
The doves have found their place to sing —
the earth remembers everything.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the winter's gone, the grey gave way.
Open up your bolted chest,
love has come to give you rest.
Come away, come away —
you've been hiding long enough today.

[Verse 2]

I traced my name into the frost
and counted everything I'd lost.
The paired birds settled on the eave
and sang of all that dared believe.

You didn't ask me to explain,
you only stood there in the lane.
And something deep inside my core
remembered what I'd sealed the door for.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the winter's gone, the grey gave way.
Love was patient at your door,
you don't have to hide no more.
Come away, come away —
the flowers came while you were away.

[Bridge]

I was the frost-cracked, silent field,
you were the bloom that would not yield —
breaking open ground in me
until the fig tree stood up free.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the season turned, I couldn't stay.
I'm stepping out beyond the wall I've made —
love was never far, love never strayed.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
