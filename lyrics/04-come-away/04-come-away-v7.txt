Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I climbed up to the attic, shut the hatch —
the bare branches scratched and scratched and scratched.
I turned the lamp down low until it died
and told the morning it could wait outside.

But you came up the stair and called my name —
your voice like lamplight, steady, warm as flame:
"The branches hold their first small bud again —
come on down, beloved, winter's in the rain."

[Pre-Chorus]

The bare limbs tremble into something green,
the finest morning anybody's seen.
The sparrows in the eaves begin to call —
the vineyard wakes and first light touches all.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the attic dust has had its day.
The lamp has caught and throws its gold below —
come down to where the morning wants to go.
Come away, come away —
the branches know a better way.

[Verse 2]

I pressed against the attic pane to see
the bare limbs stir and stir in front of me,
the first light catching every frozen bough
and making something like a promise now.

You left the lamp burning in the hall —
a steady flame to lead me through it all.
You stood below, unhurried, unafraid,
patient as the dawn the first light made.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the bare limbs hold a kind of grace.
The first light caught the branches, gold and still —
come down to me, I'm waiting at the sill.
Come away, come away —
I left the lamp to mark the place.

[Bridge]

I was the bare branch stripped of every leaf,
you were the first light after all that grief —
steady through the dark of my unlit years
until the smallest green thing could appear.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the bare and brittle season fell away.
I'm stepping out from attic dust to ground —
love was never bare, love can still be found.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready — I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
