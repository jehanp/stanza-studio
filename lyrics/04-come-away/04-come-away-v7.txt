Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I climbed into the attic with October,
pulled the hatch above me, went to ground.
Kept the lamp extinguished in the rafters —
told myself I didn't need to be found.

But under the hatch your voice came up like morning,
gentle as the light beneath the door:
"The bare branches are budding, love, already —
come down, come see — there's something here worth more."

[Pre-Chorus]

The almond trees are white along the hillside,
the fig tree's putting out its tender leaf.
I hear a voice that climbed the stairs to find me —
a sound that's sweeter than my old relief.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the dark is thinned, the first light's on its way.
Lift the hatch you've held shut tight,
love has climbed the stairs to bring you light.
Come away, come away —
the branches budded quiet while you lay.

[Verse 2]

I pressed my hand along the attic floorboards,
felt the warmth come rising from below.
The birds outside were calling through the rafters —
a world that didn't wait for me to go.

You didn't bang the hatch, you didn't push your way —
you just held the lamp below the crack.
And something cold and careful in my chest remembered
that I'd always meant to find my way back.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the dark is thinned, the first light's here to stay.
Love stood patient at the stair,
you don't have to hide alone up there.
Come away, come away —
the world woke up while you were locked away.

[Bridge]

I was the bare and leafless branch in deep December,
you were the first light offering its warmth —
pressing through the cold boards of my hiding
until the bud inside me turned toward north.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the long dark lifted and I couldn't stay.
I'm climbing down through all I thought I'd keep —
love was never lost, love was never sleep.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready — I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
