Come Away

Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop

[Verse 1]

I locked the cellar door the drought year's ending,
stacked the empty jars and swore I'd stay.
Told myself that waiting down in darkness
was the safest way to let the dry months fade.

But your voice came down the stair like water in a cistern,
gentle as the rain that knew exactly where to fall:
"The creek has come back, love — the wells are filling —
come up and hear the rush of the waterfall."

[Pre-Chorus]

The vines are climbing thick above the trellis,
the figs are pressing sweet against the vine.
The swallows dip and wheel across the valley —
the dry has broken into something fine.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the drought has gone, the cistern's full today.
Open up your cellar door,
love is flooding in from every shore.
Come away, come away —
the creek is singing past your door today.

[Verse 2]

I pressed my ear against the cool stone ceiling,
heard the water pushing through the clay.
The first thin trickle made its way toward daylight —
the crack of thirst had found a way.

You didn't come to pry the timber loose —
you just kept standing in the rain above.
And something dried inside my chest remembered
that it knew the taste of running water, knew the taste of love.

[Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the long dry spell has had its last full day.
Love kept raining on your roof,
you don't need more drought as proof.
Come away, come away —
the water's deep and fresh and running your way.

[Bridge]

I was the cracked and sunbaked creek bed in July,
you were the rain that came back nonetheless —
pressing through the hardpan of my drought years
until the water table answered yes.

[Final Chorus]

Come away, come away —
the dry ground broke and I could not delay.
I'm stepping up through everything I barred —
love was never scarce, love was never hard.
Come away, come away —
I think I'm ready — I think I'm ready for the day.

[Vamp]

Come away... come away...
The door is open now — the door is open.
Come away... come away...

[Outro]

The rain is over, the rain is gone —
the morning smells like moving on.
