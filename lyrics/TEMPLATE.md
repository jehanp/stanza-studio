## Genre-Specific Structural Alternatives

Not every song needs to follow the base structure above. The sections below can replace or supplement the standard ones to give each song a shape that fits its genre.

| Section | Replaces / Supplements | Best For | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Chorus | Hip-Hop, R&B | A shorter, punchier repeating phrase — the line that sticks |
| **Refrain** | _(adds between verses)_ | Blues, Folk, Americana | A short, recurring fragment (1–2 lines) that haunts the song |
| **Build** | Bridge | Electronic, Synth-Pop | Rising tension — stripped-back lyrics that set up the Drop |
| **Drop** | _(adds after Build)_ | Electronic, Synth-Pop | The payoff — high-energy, hook-driven, beat-forward |
| **Vamp** | _(adds before Outro)_ | Neo-Soul, Jazz, Gospel | A repeated groove with vocal improvisation or ad-libs |
| **Instrumental** | _(adds anywhere)_ | Prog Rock, Art Rock, Post-Rock | A wordless passage — stage directions describe the mood |
| **Interlude** | _(adds between sections)_ | Hip-Hop, Ambient, Cinematic | A brief spoken or atmospheric moment between movements |
| **Call and Response** | Verse or Chorus | Gospel, Soul, Folk | Two voices trading lines — congregation-style or duet |
| **Tag** | _(adds after Final Chorus)_ | Pop, Soul, Country | A repeated final phrase that fades or trails off |
| **Spoken Word** | Verse or Bridge | Hip-Hop, Spoken Word, Folk | A section delivered as speech rather than sung |

### Example: Electronic / Synth-Pop Structure

> Verse 1 → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus → **Build** → **Drop** → Final Chorus → Outro

### Example: Hip-Hop / Spoken Word Structure

> Verse 1 → **Hook** → Verse 2 → **Interlude** → **Hook** → Bridge _(spoken)_ → **Final Hook** → Outro

### Example: Dark Folk / Blues Americana Structure

> Verse 1 → Verse 2 → **Refrain** → Chorus → Verse 3 → Chorus → Bridge → Final Chorus → Outro

### Example: Neo-Soul / Jazzy Pop Structure

> Verse 1 → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus → Bridge → Final Chorus → **Vamp** → Outro

### Example: Progressive Rock / Art Rock Structure

> Verse 1 → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus → Bridge → **Instrumental** → Final Chorus → Outro
