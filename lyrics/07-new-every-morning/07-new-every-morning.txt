New Every Morning

Ambient / Dream Pop

[Verse 1]

The dark won't quit; these walls can say my name,
each hour draws a map of every hidden shame.
Bitter metal settles heavy on my tongue,
and last night's broken anthem still gets sung.

[Verse 2]

I replay every door I slammed too fast,
each word I failed to shelter from the blast.
My soul bows low beneath the wormwood weight,
and gall still burns for sentences too late.

[Pre-Chorus]

But I remember - under splinters, under flood,
there is a mercy deeper than my blood.

[Chorus]

Morning, morning — it arrives like mercy,
quiet through the curtains, empty hands.
Morning, morning — faithful as a heartbeat,
new before confession forms or stands.
The compassion doesn't fail,
it just comes back — it just comes back.

[Verse 3]

I tried to earn the dawn and call it fair,
I balanced ledgers, choking on the prayer.
But light came back before my sums were done,
it rose unbought, like grace that owes me none.

[Pre-Chorus]

So I wait - not because I'm patient, not because I'm strong,
but because stillness is the one place I can tell the truth of wrong.

[Chorus]

Morning, morning — it arrives like mercy,
gentle on the floorboards where I fell.
Morning, morning — faithful as a promise
spoken by the One who cannot fail.
The compassion doesn't end,
it just begins — it just begins.

[Bridge]

Great is the faithfulness I cannot claim,
yet every dawn still calls me by my name.
It does not start by reading out my list,
it starts with breath and says, "You still exist."

[Final Chorus]

Morning, morning — new and undeserved,
clean mercy laid against my open palms.
Morning, morning — I don't have to earn it —
it arrives, it arrives, it arrives.

[Outro]

The sun keeps no archive of the dark.
