New Every Morning

Ambient / Dream Pop

[Verse 1]

Three a.m. again — the ceiling knows my name,
every crack a map of all the places I went wrong.
The bitterness is sitting on my tongue like copper,
and the night is singing yesterday's same tired song.

[Verse 2]

I replay every door I closed too hard,
every word I left out in the rain.
My soul is bent beneath the weight of wormwood,
and the gall of what I'll never get to say again.

[Pre-Chorus]

But I remember — somewhere underneath the wreckage,
there's a reason I haven't shut my eyes for good.

[Chorus]

Morning, morning — it arrives like mercy,
quiet through the curtains, no demand.
Morning, morning — faithful as a heartbeat,
new before I've even raised my hands.
The compassion doesn't fail,
it just comes back — it just comes back.

[Verse 3]

I tried to earn the dawn, I tried to make it fair,
balanced every ledger, prayed the numbers would add up.
But the light returned before I finished counting —
it didn't wait for me to fill the cup.

[Pre-Chorus]

So I wait — not because I'm patient,
but because the stillness is the only honest thing I've got.

[Chorus]

Morning, morning — it arrives like mercy,
gentle on the floorboards where I fell.
Morning, morning — faithful as a promise
made by someone who has never learned to fail.
The compassion doesn't end,
it just begins — it just begins.

[Bridge]

Great is the faithfulness I cannot name,
the one that shows up wearing light.
It doesn't ask me what I've done —
it only says, "You made it through the night."

[Final Chorus]

Morning, morning — new and undeserved,
a clean page pressed against my open palms.
Morning, morning — I don't have to earn it —
it arrives, it arrives, it arrives.

[Outro]

The sun doesn't keep a record of the dark.
