Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I stepped beyond the last of what I'd mapped,
where the asphalt cracked apart and the desert wind unwrapped.
Something drew me through a threshold in the sand —
there was water out there, cool and still, and someone took my hand.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the creek ran soft, didn't flood, didn't fight —
it just moved like mercy slipping through the night.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a fire in the open and a seat kept warm for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the wasteland, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a hollow in the rock where the wind forgets to howl,
and I could've sworn I heard my name beneath the desert owl.
But a shade I hadn't made fell across my burning face,
and the grief that used to stalk me couldn't keep my pace.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a fire in the open and a seat kept warm for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the wasteland, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Water on my forehead, I don't know whose spring —
mercy at my shoulder like it knew about everything.
Every threshold I have crossed still holds the light I found,
every road I thought was dying rose up solid from the ground.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a fire in the open and it's burning for you and me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the wasteland, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
