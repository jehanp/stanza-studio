Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I followed where the garden met the hollow and the root,
where the river ran so slowly it forgot it was en route.
Something guided me beside the water, unannounced —
there were meadows past the thicket, green and still and pronounced.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the current never pushed and never pulled too hard —
it just moved like it was keeping watch and letting down its guard.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the river with a seat beside for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a hollow in the bank where the torchlight doesn't bend,
and I swore I heard my name where the willows have no end.
But an ointment I can't trace touched the places that were sore,
and the grief that used to guard the gate can't find the gate anymore.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the river with a seat beside for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Ointment on my forehead, from the hands of the unseen —
mercy at my elbow like it always knew my need.
Every garden I have rested in still keeps the torch I left,
every hollow I thought empty was the fullest thing I'd met.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the river and it's set for you and me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the valley, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
