Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I drifted past the last light I could name,
where the river turned to current and the banks went up in flame.
Something steered me through the bend I couldn't see —
there were quiet shores out there, and the tide was saving me.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the water didn't surge, didn't swallow, didn't drown —
it just cradled me like stillness wearing a crown.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a harbour in the darkness with a lantern lit for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost beneath the water, but I'm not drifting alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a channel where the current cuts beneath the noise,
and I heard my name come back to me through the river's voice.
But a hand I hadn't asked for moved the stones beneath my feet,
and the wave that had been chasing me admitted its defeat.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a harbour in the darkness with a lantern lit for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost beneath the water, but I'm not drifting alone.

[Bridge]

Salt on my forehead, I don't know whose sea —
mercy at the shoreline like it always expected me.
Every harbour I have sheltered in still has my name on the wall,
every tide I thought would take me was just answering a call.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a harbour in the darkness and it's open for us all.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost beneath the water, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
