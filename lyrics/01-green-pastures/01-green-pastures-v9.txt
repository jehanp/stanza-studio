Green Pastures

Indie Folk / Acoustic

[Verse 1]

I followed the frost to the edge of the known,
where the streetlamps drowned in fog and the path was overgrown.
Something moved ahead of me without a sound or name —
there was open ground out there, thawing out, and it was glad I came.

[Pre-Chorus]

And the stream ran clear but never in a rush —
like patience wearing water, moving soft and hush.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the window with the candle lit for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the winter, but I'm not walking alone.

[Verse 2]

There's a crossing in the fog where the cold knows every name,
and I nearly thought the silence was the one who was to blame.
But a warmth I never lit kept the frost from my door,
and the fear that had been hunting me had no keys anymore.

[Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the window with the candle lit for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the winter, but I'm not walking alone.

[Bridge]

Dew on my forehead, I don't know whose morning —
kindness at my shoulder like it never needed warning.
Every winter road I've wandered still has footprints in the snow,
every path I thought was frozen was just the long way home.

[Final Chorus]

I don't need a thing, I don't need a thing —
there's a table by the window and the chair is set for me.
Pour it out until it spills, let it overflow —
I was lost inside the winter, but I was never on my own.

[Outro]

Green pastures in the rearview,
green pastures up ahead.
