## Raga

A **raga** is not a scale — it is a complete melodic personality. It defines which notes to use, which to avoid, which to emphasize, and how to move between them. Each raga has its own emotional identity (called **rasa**) and is traditionally associated with a time of day or season.

### Core Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Aroha** | Ascending pattern of notes (going up) |
| **Avaroha** | Descending pattern of notes (going down) |
| **Vadi** | The most important, most-played note — the "king" note |
| **Samvadi** | The second most important note — the "minister" note |
| **Pakad** | The signature phrase that defines the raga — its fingerprint |
| **Gamak** | Heavy oscillation/shake between notes |
| **Meend** | A smooth glide between two notes (like a slide) |
| **Andolan** | A slow, gentle oscillation on a specific note |
| **Shruti** | Microtones — Indian music uses 22 microtonal divisions per octave (vs. Western 12) |
| **Komal** | Flat note (lowered) |
| **Tivra** | Sharp note (raised) |
| **Shuddha** | Natural/pure note |

### Structure of a Raga Performance

- **Alap** — Free, slow improvisation with no rhythm. Explores the raga's mood and introduces notes gradually. No percussion.
- **Jod** — Adds a rhythmic pulse, still no tabla.
- **Jhala** — Fast, rhythmic phase. Intense strumming patterns.
- **Gat / Bandish** — A composed piece (the "song") played with tabla accompaniment. Improvisation happens around this.
- **Taan** — Fast melodic runs, often cascading. A show of virtuosity.

### Common Ragas (to know)

| Raga | Time | Mood / Rasa |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Bhairav** | Dawn | Devotional, serious, ancient |
| **Bhairavi** | Morning / closing | Emotional, devotional, melancholic |
| **Yaman** | Evening | Romantic, peaceful, longing |
| **Darbari Kanhada** | Late night | Deep, serious, majestic, heavy |
| **Bhupali** | Evening | Peaceful, joyful, pentatonic |
| **Kafi** | Midnight | Devotional, sensuous, folk-adjacent |
| **Todi** | Morning | Intense, pensive, complex |
| **Bageshri** | Midnight | Longing, gentle, quiet heartache |

### Instruments Associated with Raga

- **Sitar** — Plucked string instrument with sympathetic strings (Ravi Shankar)
- **Sarod** — Fretless plucked instrument, darker and moodier than sitar
- **Bansuri** — Bamboo flute (Hariprasad Chaurasia)
- **Sarangi** — Bowed string instrument, expressive
- **Veena** — Ancient South Indian plucked string
- **Tabla** — Pair of hand drums; provides rhythm
- **Tanpura / Tambura** — Drone instrument — holds a continuous harmonic foundation
- **Harmonium** — Keyboard drone/melodic instrument, common in devotional music

### Vocal Styles in Raga

| Style | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Khayal** | The dominant Hindustani classical vocal form. Slow (vilambit) or fast (drut). Expansive improvisation around a composed piece. Most ornamented. |
| **Dhrupad** | Ancient, austere, serious. Minimal ornament, maximum gravity. Pre-dates khayal. |
| **Thumri** | Semi-classical. More emotional and expressive than khayal. Lighter, often romantic or devotional. |
| **Bhajan** | Devotional song. Repetitive, yearning, community-focused. Folk-classical blend. |
| **Qawwali** | Sufi devotional music — passionate, ecstatic, call-and-response. (Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan) |

### Key Vocal Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Melismatic** | Many notes sung across a single syllable. The defining feature of Indian classical vocals vs. Western syllabic singing. |
| **Meend (in voice)** | A smooth glide from one note to another — not a jump, a slide |
| **Gamak (in voice)** | Heavy, rapid oscillation/shake between two notes |
| **Murki** | A very quick ornament — like a grace note cluster |
| **Khatka** | A sharp, quick flick of a note |
| **Andolan (in voice)** | Slow, gentle wavering on a specific note — not vibrato, more intentional |
| **Sa** | The tonic — the "home" note. Indian music doesn't fix this to a pitch; each performer sets their own Sa. |

### Key Feeling to Capture

- Notes bend into each other — nothing is "straight"
- Drone underneath everything (usually Sa and Pa — tonic and fifth)
- Silence and space are as important as the notes
- Music builds slowly; tension releases in waves
- **Vocals are highly ornamented** — the melody moves between syllables, not just on them

## Rock

**Rock** is guitar-driven music built on electric instruments, amplification, and a strong backbeat (snare on beats 2 and 4).

### Core Characteristics

- Electric guitar (rhythm + lead), electric bass, drum kit
- 4/4 time is most common
- **Power chords** — 2- or 3-note chords (root + fifth) for a big, open sound
- Verse-chorus song structure
- Guitar solos
- Distortion and overdrive

### Subgenres (quick reference)

| Genre | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| **Classic Rock** | Melodic, clean-to-crunch guitar (Led Zeppelin, The Who, CCR) |
| **Hard Rock** | Heavier distortion, bigger riffs, arena energy (AC/DC, Deep Purple) |
| **Alternative Rock** | Broader sound palette, emotional range, less formula (REM, U2, Pixies) |
| **Indie Rock** | DIY aesthetic, jangly or raw guitars, introspective |
| **Psychedelic Rock** | Effects-heavy, modal, exploratory (Pink Floyd early, Hendrix) |

## Grunge

**Grunge** emerged from Seattle in the late 1980s and broke mainstream around 1991. It fused the heaviness of metal with the rawness and anti-commercialism of punk.

### Defining Artists

- **Nirvana** — Melodic vocals over explosive loud-quiet-loud dynamics
- **Soundgarden** — Heavy, tuned-down riffs, modal and dark, powerful vocals (Chris Cornell)
- **Pearl Jam** — Emotional, bluesy, anthemic
- **Alice in Chains** — Darkest of the four, heavy harmonies, sludgy riffs
- **Mudhoney** — Raw proto-grunge, garage-rock edge

### Core Characteristics

- **Distortion** — Heavy, fuzzy, often with a Big Muff or similar pedal
- **Drop tuning** — Guitars tuned down (Drop D, Eb standard, D standard) for a heavier, looser feel
- **Loud-quiet-loud** — Quiet verse, explosive chorus (Nirvana's signature)
- **Feedback and noise** — Deliberate dissonance and squealing harmonics
- **Angst and alienation** — Lyrically raw, confessional, anti-hero
- **Lo-fi / unpolished production** — Intentional roughness, not overproduced
- **Tempo** — Often mid-tempo; not fast thrash, not slow doom. Heavy and lumbering or driving.
- **Vocals** — Range from melodic to screaming; often strained, cracked, expressive

### Guitar Techniques in Grunge

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Palm muting** | Right hand rests on strings near bridge; creates chunky, percussive attack |
| **Open-string riffs** | Using open strings in riffs adds resonance and slack |
| **Pinch harmonics** | Squealing overtone notes |
| **Dive bombs** | Tremolo arm drops pitch down |
| **Power chords** | Core of rhythm guitar |

## Dark Folk

**Dark folk** is an acoustic-rooted genre that blends traditional folk instrumentation with heavy lyrical themes — mortality, grief, spiritual longing, mythology, and the shadow side of human experience. It strips away production gloss and lets discomfort breathe.

### Core Characteristics

- Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked, often sparse and deliberate)
- Minor keys and modal scales — Dorian and Aeolian are common
- **Drone** — a held or repeated low note underneath melody, creating unresolved tension
- Tempo is slow to mid-tempo; space and silence are used intentionally
- Vocals are often close-miked, intimate, sometimes raw or wavering
- Lyrics lean toward allegory, nature imagery, and spiritual weight
- Minimal production — room sound, ambient breath, natural decay

### Mood and Feel

- Somber but not nihilistic — grief as reverence
- Ancient-feeling — timeless rather than contemporary
- Close, like a story told by firelight
- Tension that doesn't always resolve; endings can feel open or unfinished

### Key Artists

- **Nick Drake** — Sparse, introspective, luminous even in darkness
- **Current 93** — Experimental edge, apocalyptic imagery, biblical undertones
- **Wovenhand** — Post-punk influence, devotional intensity, desert spirituality
- **Timber Timbre** — Gothic atmosphere, warm and unsettling at once
- **Alasdair Roberts** — Scottish traditional influence, modal and austere

### Instruments

| Instrument | Role |
|------------|------|
| **Acoustic guitar** | Primary melodic and rhythmic voice |
| **Cello / viola** | Adds mournful depth beneath vocals |
| **Banjo** | Clawhammer style for droning, cyclical feel |
| **Fiddle** | Often melancholic; more wailing than bright |
| **Harmonium / pump organ** | Drone and hymn-like underpinning |
| **Upright bass** | Low, resonant, restrained |

### Lyrical Style

- Imagery over statement — the feeling is buried in metaphor
- Nature as symbol (mountains, rivers, dust, seasons)
- Mortality framed through the natural world
- Spiritual crisis or surrender, rarely resolution
- Short, dense lines carrying compressed weight

## Americana

**Americana** is a broad American roots genre that draws from country, folk, blues, gospel, and rock without fully committing to any one of them. It values authenticity, storytelling, and emotional honesty over commercial polish.

### Core Characteristics

- Acoustic and electric guitars, often in the same arrangement
- Pedal steel guitar — the defining sonic signature of the genre
- Fiddle, mandolin, banjo, upright or electric bass
- 4/4 time; backbeat present but unhurried
- Production is warm and organic — analogue-sounding, room-alive
- Vocals are expressive and plain-spoken; no vocal gymnastics

### Mood and Feel

- Earthy and human — nothing is too perfect
- Road-worn, lived-in, nostalgic without being sentimental
- Stories of loss, work, home, faith, and the land
- Wide open spaces — sonically and emotionally
- Can swing from tender to raw, quiet to full-band

### Key Artists

- **Gillian Welch** — Stark, devastating folk-country. Minimal arrangement, maximal weight.
- **Townes Van Zandt** — Heartbroken storytelling, bluesy fingerpicking, poetic fatalism
- **Jason Isbell** — Literary lyrics, rock energy under country bones
- **Nanci Griffith** — Warm, narrative, acoustic-forward
- **Emmylou Harris** — Voice as instrument, deeply rooted in tradition

### Key Sounds

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Pedal steel** | A lap-played electric instrument; signature "crying" or "sighing" glides |
| **Fingerpicking** | Travis-style or alternating bass; keeps rhythm and melody together |
| **Dobro / resonator guitar** | Metallic, mournful, Delta blues ancestry |
| **Lap steel** | Cousin to pedal steel; simpler, more droning |
| **Hammond B3 / piano** | When present, adds warmth and gospel roots |

### Lyrical Style

- Plain speech elevated by rhythm and image
- Concrete detail — specific places, objects, names
- Storyline and character — often a third-person narrative
- Faith appears without preaching; doubt appears without despair
- The chorus arrives like a truth the verse was circling toward
